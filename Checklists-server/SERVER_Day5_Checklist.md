## TRAINING CHECKLIST

**Today's dynamic: Trainee runs their section. Trainer shadows, observes, and coaches after, not during.**

### 1. Full-Service Execution
- [ ] Trainee manages their full section independently from open to close
- [ ] Complete service cycle on every table: greet, drink order, apps, entrées, check-back, dessert/coffee, check, farewell
- [ ] Manage pacing across multiple tables without trainer intervention
- [ ] "This is the real thing. Show me you can run your section without me."

### 2. Guest Interaction & Upselling
- [ ] Trainee handles all guest interaction: greetings, recommendations, complaints, special requests
- [ ] Upselling execution: suggest appetizers, premium drinks, desserts naturally during service
- [ ] Trainer observes tone, confidence, menu knowledge, and recovery from curveballs
- [ ] "Confidence builds trust. Guests take your lead when you know the menu inside out."

### 3. Team Coordination & Communication
- [ ] Work seamlessly with kitchen, bartenders, food runners, hosts, and fellow servers
- [ ] Practice hand-offs: covering tables, assisting with large parties, communicating 86'd items
- [ ] Proactive communication. Don't wait to be asked.

### 4. Advanced POS & Problem Solving
- [ ] Trainee handles all POS operations for the entire shift. No trainer assistance.
- [ ] Complex scenarios: split checks, large party tabs, comps/voids (MOD approval), gift cards, billing disputes
- [ ] Trainer creates pressure: rapid table turns, curveball modifications, guest role-play complaints
- [ ] If Toast still feels slow, flag it. This needs to be resolved before mock service.

### 5. End-of-Shift Responsibilities
- [ ] Trainee leads shift wrap-up: Jolt checklist, table resets, side work, section breakdown
- [ ] Shift reports: run shift review on Toast, print, staple credit slips, submit to manager
- [ ] Verify all tasks complete, inform MOD, confirm no outstanding items before clocking out
- [ ] "Leave your section the way you'd want to find it at the start of your next shift."
