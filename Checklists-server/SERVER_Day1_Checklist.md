## ORIENTATION BLOCK

### 1. Welcome & Introduction
- [ ] Meet the Manager on Duty. Introduce trainee to leadership on shift.
- [ ] Complete any remaining new hire paperwork
- [ ] Clock in. Walk trainee through the time clock process.
- [ ] 5-Day Program Overview: explain the training structure, what each day covers, and what's expected by Day 5
- [ ] Set the tone: "This is your career development, not just a job checklist. Own your training."

### 2. Trainual Accountability Check
- [ ] Confirm trainee has completed all pre-training Trainual modules (Onboarding Roadmap)
- [ ] Review: Welcome to Trainual, Welcome Packet, Employee Handbook, Role-Specific Manual
- [ ] If incomplete, trainee must finish before Day 2. Flag to management immediately.

### 3. Company Policies & Procedures
- [ ] Mission, Vision, and Core Values. What TPH stands for and how it shows up on the floor.
- [ ] Attendance policy: call-out procedures, no-shows, tardiness expectations
- [ ] Uniform policy: what's required, what's not acceptable, where to get replacements
- [ ] Code of conduct: professionalism, phone policy, guest-first mentality

### 4. Uniforms, Hygiene & Safety
- [ ] Appearance standards walkthrough: hair, nails, jewelry, tattoos, fragrance
- [ ] Alcohol agreement review and signature. Responsible service expectations.
- [ ] Health and hygiene requirements: handwashing, illness reporting, food safety basics
- [ ] Emergency procedures: fire exits, first aid kit location, emergency contacts

### 5. Payroll, Tips & Scheduling
- [ ] 7Shifts: download app, log in, show how to view schedule, request time off, swap shifts
- [ ] Branch: set up account for tip payouts, explain payout cadence
- [ ] Tip structure: how tips are calculated, pooled vs. individual, tip-out percentages per role
- [ ] Pay periods, direct deposit confirmation, who to contact for payroll issues

### 6. The Grand Tour: FOH & BOH
- [ ] Front of House: entrance, host stand, dining room layout, bar area, patio/outdoor seating, restrooms
- [ ] Back of House: kitchen line, dish pit, dry storage, walk-in coolers, office, staff area, lockers
- [ ] Emergency exits. Point out every exit and the evacuation route.
- [ ] Introduce trainee to every team member on shift by name and role

## ROLE-SPECIFIC: SERVER FOUNDATIONS

### 7. Customer Interaction & Experience
- [ ] First impressions: how to greet a table (timing, body language, eye contact, energy)
- [ ] Reading the guest. Business dinner vs. date night vs. birthday vs. casual. Adjust your approach
- [ ] Handling waitlists: how to communicate wait times honestly and keep guests engaged
- [ ] Phone etiquette: how to answer the phone, take reservations, handle to-go inquiries

### 8. Opening Procedures
- [ ] Jolt app walkthrough: daily opening checklist, how to complete and submit
- [ ] Pre-shift responsibilities: table setup, condiment check, silverware roll, section readiness
- [ ] Side work assignments: what they are, how they rotate, and why they matter
- [ ] "Your section should be guest-ready before the first table sits down. No exceptions."

### 9. Table Management
- [ ] Floor plan walkthrough: table numbers, section assignments, where each section starts and ends
- [ ] Table types: 2-tops, 4-tops, booths, high-tops, patio, large party configurations
- [ ] Rotation awareness: how tables are assigned through the host, how to stay in rotation
- [ ] Capacity and covers: what's the max per section, how to manage a full section without drowning

### 10. Clearing Tables & Service Etiquette
- [ ] When to clear: read the table, don't rush, don't let plates stack up
- [ ] How to clear: right side, one hand or two, never scrape plates in front of guests
- [ ] Crumbing the table between courses when applicable
- [ ] Resetting for the next course or the next guest. Speed and cleanliness matter equally
- [ ] Pre-bussing: keep the table clean throughout the meal, not just at the end

### 11. Guest Interaction: Observe Trainer
- [ ] Trainer walks trainee through a mock greeting at an empty table. Demonstrate the full approach
- [ ] Walk through the steps of service verbally: greet, drink order, appetizer suggestion, entrée order, check back, dessert/coffee offer, drop check, thank and close
- [ ] Explain upselling: "It's not about pushing product. It's about reading what the guest wants and offering something better."
- [ ] Trainee observes. No live table interaction yet (that's Day 2)

### 12. POS System Introduction: Toast
- [ ] Log in to Toast. Trainee creates their profile, sets their PIN
- [ ] Navigate the menu: categories, modifiers, special instructions, coursing
- [ ] Practice ringing in a mock order: appetizer, entrée with mods, drink, dessert
- [ ] How to split checks, transfer tables, apply discounts (manager approval required)
- [ ] Closing out a check: cash, card, gift card, comps
- [ ] "Speed on the POS comes from knowing where everything is. Practice until it's muscle memory."

### 13. Menu Familiarization
- [ ] Walk through the full food menu section by section (appetizers, salads, entrées, sides, desserts)
- [ ] Highlight top sellers, chef specials, and seasonal items
- [ ] Allergen awareness: what contains gluten, dairy, nuts, shellfish. "If you don't know, ask the kitchen. Never guess."
- [ ] Drink menu overview: signature cocktails, wine by the glass, beer list, non-alcoholic options
- [ ] "By Day 5, a guest should be able to ask you about anything on this menu and get a confident answer."

### 14. Common Modifications
- [ ] Most frequent mods: dressing on the side, no onion, gluten-free, extra spicy, allergy accommodations
- [ ] How to ring mods into Toast: where the buttons are, how to write special instructions
- [ ] Kitchen communication: when a mod requires a verbal fire vs. just a ticket note
- [ ] "Get it right the first time. Re-fires cost the kitchen time and cost you trust."

### 15. Service Observation
- [ ] Walk the dining room during live service (15-20 minutes toward end of shift)
- [ ] Point out real-time examples: how servers approach tables, how food runners deliver, how bussers reset
- [ ] Watch the flow: guest seated → greeted → drinks → apps → entrées → check → table turned
- [ ] Trainee watches the rhythm. "This is what your Day 2 looks like. Tomorrow you're next to me doing this."

### 16. Notes Section: iPad
- [ ] Show trainee where to log daily notes (iPad or designated device)
- [ ] Explain what to write: key takeaways, questions for tomorrow, anything they want to review
- [ ] "Your notes are for you. The more you write down now, the less you'll forget later."
