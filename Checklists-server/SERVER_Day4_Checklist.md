## TRAINING CHECKLIST

### 1. Cross-Training Expo Session #2
- [ ] Recap from Session #1: station setup, expediting, quality control, kitchen communication
- [ ] More independent than Session #1. Trainee manages the expo window.
- [ ] Once complete, return to assigned trainer for remainder of shift

### 2. Mid-Shift Service Execution
- [ ] Trainee works their section with increasing independence. Trainer shadows.
- [ ] Handle guest interaction, order accuracy, table turns, and side work simultaneously
- [ ] "By now, the basics should be automatic. Show me your service flow."

### 3. Guest Relations & Personalized Service
- [ ] Exceeding expectations: remembering names, acknowledging occasions, anticipating needs
- [ ] Active listening: read tone, body language, and energy. Adjust your approach.
- [ ] Special occasions and large party service: coordination, pacing, six-item rule, communication with kitchen
- [ ] Cultural sensitivity: inclusive service for all guests regardless of background, dietary norms, or preferences
- [ ] "Guest service extends beyond taking orders. It's about building relationships."

### 4. Handling Complaints & Difficult Situations
- [ ] Role-play: food quality complaint, long wait time, billing dispute, intoxicated guest
- [ ] Trainee handles it. Trainer debriefs after each scenario.
- [ ] Review company policies on refunds, comps, and service refusals
- [ ] "Own the problem. Offer a solution. Follow up."

### 5. Closing Procedures
- [ ] Trainee leads closing: Jolt checklist, table resets, section breakdown, side work completion
- [ ] Closing reports: run shift review on Toast, print, staple credit slips, submit to manager
- [ ] Trainer observes and notes anything missed or done out of order
- [ ] "A clean close sets up the next shift for success."
