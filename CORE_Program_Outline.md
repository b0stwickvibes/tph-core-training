## SECTION 1: THE DOCUMENT SYSTEM

### How It Works Now — And Why It's Not Enough

Right now, each role has two parallel documents: a Trainer Manual (comprehensive teaching guide) and a Trainee Manual (checklist with pro tips). The Trainer Manual has everything — how to demonstrate, what to explain, what to quiz on, what mistakes to look for. The Trainee Manual covers the same topics but only gives 1-2 sentences per step.

**Here's the reality:**
- Trainers aren't pulling up a 1,500-line document while they're working a live shift with a trainee standing next to them. They need to already *know* how to train — the document just needs to remind them what to cover and in what order.
- Trainees go home after a training shift with a checklist that says "learned about cocktail structure" but no actual content explaining what cocktail structure *is*. All the substance lives in the Trainer Manual — which the trainee never sees.
- Maintaining two parallel documents means every update happens twice. That's inefficient and it drifts.

**The fix isn't one massive combined document.** It's three purpose-built layers, each designed for how it's actually used.

---

### Layer 1: Trainer Retraining Session — Led by Me

**What this is:** I take the current Trainer Manual and use it as my curriculum to physically train each trainer. Not a meeting. Not a read-through. I play the trainee. They demonstrate how they'd run training. I coach in real time.

**Duration:** 90-120 minutes per trainer

**This is where the depth lives.** The Trainer Manual's 1,500 lines of detailed instruction? That's what *I* use to prepare and deliver this session. The trainer doesn't need to memorize it — they need to *experience* it with me, the same way their trainee will experience it with them.

**Session Breakdown:**

| Block | Time | What We're Doing |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| **Set the Standard** | 10 min | Why we're doing this. What's changed. What I expect going forward. No ambiguity. |
| **Daily Checklist Walkthrough** | 15 min | Review the new floor checklists. What "covering a topic" actually means — not just mentioning it, but demonstrating it, having the trainee practice it, and confirming they got it. |
| **Opening Procedures — Live Demo** | 15 min | Trainer walks me through a full bar/server/host opening as if I'm Day 1. I observe, coach, and correct. |
| **Closing Procedures — Live Demo** | 15 min | Same format. Trainer demonstrates closing while teaching me. |
| **Steps of Service Execution** | 15 min | How to teach the service flow — greeting, table touch cadence, check presentation, the full sequence. Not just "tell them about steps of service" — show them how to *teach* it. |
| **Upselling & Product Knowledge** | 10 min | When to introduce upselling during training. How to make it consultative, not scripted. "Knowledge is not power. Actionable and executed knowledge is power — confidence is key." |
| **Accountability & Check-Ins** | 10 min | The new form requirements. Checklist photo upload. What happens if submissions are missed. Incentive structure — crystal clear. |
| **30-Day Mentorship** | 5 min | Post-training touchpoints. What the Day 30 evaluation looks like. How their full payout depends on the trainee's performance, not just showing up for 5 shifts. |
| **Q&A + Close** | 5-10 min | Open floor. Confirm every trainer walks out knowing exactly what's expected. |

**What the trainer walks away with:** They've physically practiced executing training under my observation. The standard has been demonstrated, not assumed. They have their daily floor checklists. They know the accountability requirements. No gray area.

### Layer 2: Daily Floor Checklist — The Trainer's Shift Tool

**What this is:** One page per training day. The trainer carries it during the shift. Topics to cover, order to cover them, key reminders — no instructional paragraphs. Check it off as you go. Sign it at the end. Photograph it. Upload it with the accountability form.

**Format:**

```
[ROLE] — DAY [#] TRAINING CHECKLIST
Date: _______ | Trainer: _______ | Trainee: _______

□ TOPIC: [Name]
  → Key point
  → Key point

□ TOPIC: [Name]
  → Key point
  → Key point

[...continues through all daily topics...]

□ END OF SHIFT RECAP (10 min — non-negotiable)
  → Check for Understanding: [Specific question]
  → Scenario: [Specific situation prompt]
  → Preview tomorrow's focus

□ ACCOUNTABILITY FORM SUBMITTED: Y / N
□ CHECKLIST PHOTO UPLOADED: Y / N

Trainer Signature: _______ Date: _______
Trainee Signature: _______ Date: _______
```

**Design rules:**
- One page per day, per role — 15 total checklists (5 days × 3 roles)
- Checkbox format. Trainer checks off as they go. No flipping through pages.
- Key points are one-line reminders — the depth was covered in the retraining session
- End-of-shift recap is *on the checklist* — it can't be "forgotten" during a rush because it's a line item
- Dual signatures create a physical paper trail alongside the digital accountability form
- Photo upload to the accountability form = dual verification without extra admin time

### Layer 3: Enriched Trainee Manual — The Study Guide

**What this is:** The trainee's take-home reference. After they experience a topic on the floor with their trainer, they go home and study the *why* behind it. This is what makes the knowledge checks meaningful — they're testing retention of concepts the trainee both *did* and *read about*, not just one or the other.

**What's changing from the current trainee manual:**
- Current version: 1-2 sentence topic description + italicized pro tip per step
- Enriched version: Adds the educational substance — the "what" and "why" — pulled from the trainer manual. Each step uses one of three bucket templates (Skill, Process, Guest-Facing) based on what the step teaches.
- Content supports the existing Trainual knowledge checks — trainees study real substance, not just topic names
- Becomes an ongoing reference after training — not just a 5-day document

**What it does NOT become:** A copy of the Trainer Manual. No coaching methodology, no "how to teach this" language. Just the content the trainee needs to understand and retain.

**Example — Day 2, Step 7: Building a Cocktail**

*Current trainee manual says:*
> "Learn the fundamentals of cocktail structure and flavor balance. Understanding how flavors work together will help you craft well-rounded cocktails that keep guests coming back."

*Enriched version adds:*
> Every cocktail has a structure — a base spirit (the foundation: vodka, gin, tequila, whiskey, rum, or brandy), a modifier (adds complexity: vermouth, liqueurs, amari), a sweetener (balances acidity: simple syrup, agave, honey), and an acid (brightens the drink: citrus juice, shrubs). The balance between these four components is what separates a great cocktail from an average one. When something tastes "off," it's almost always one of these four being too strong or too weak. Understanding this lets you troubleshoot on the fly and adapt to guest preferences — that's what makes you dangerous behind the bar.

**Build sequence:** All three roles built and finalized. Content protocol established in `PROTOCOL_Enriched_Trainee_Manual.md`. Each step uses bucket templates (Skill / Process / Guest-Facing) with priority logic for when to use full buckets vs. sentence-only format.

## SECTION 2: ACCOUNTABILITY SYSTEM

### The Form — Upgraded

**Current state:** The accountability form exists. Some trainers fill it out. Some don't. There's no consequence either way. That stops now.

**New form includes:**
1. What did you cover today? (Maps directly to daily checklist topics)
2. Where is the trainee struggling?
3. What's the plan for tomorrow?
4. Photo upload of the signed daily floor checklist

**Submission rules:**
- Completed before the trainer leaves the building — every training shift, no exceptions
- No form + no photo = no trainer incentive for that shift
- Repeated missed submissions = conversation about whether you should be on the approved trainer list

### My Involvement — Day 1 and Day 5

I'm not doing daily debriefs with every active trainer across three locations. That's not realistic for this role and it's not necessary if the form and checklist are doing their job.

**Day 1 — I sit down with the trainer for 10-15 minutes:**
- How's the trainee looking?
- Any red flags?
- Are you following the checklist?
- Set the tone. Problems caught on Day 1 don't compound for 4 more days.

**Day 5 — Same format:**
- Is this person ready for mock service? Honestly.
- What gaps remain?
- Trainer's final read before evaluation phase.

**Days 2-4 — The form and checklist photo handle it.** I review submissions if time allows or if something flags. Red flags that pull me in early:
- Trainee struggling on the same topic for 2+ consecutive days
- Trainer consistently skipping checklist items (visible in the photo)
- Form answers that don't match what the checklist shows
- No submission at all

If none of those trigger, the system runs without me touching it mid-week.

## SECTION 3: 30-DAY POST-TRAINING MENTORSHIP

### The Structure

| Phase | When | What the Trainer Does | What I Do |
|-------|------|----------------------|-----------|
| **Training Period** | 5-day C.O.R.E. program | Executes daily checklist. Submits accountability form + photo. Conducts end-of-shift recaps. Flags concerns to me. | Day 1 and Day 5 check-ins. Monitor form submissions. Course-correct as needed. |
| **Next 30 Days** | Post-training | Available as a resource. Observes trainee when they overlap. Notes any regression or gaps. Quick touchpoints on overlapping shifts. | Periodic spot-checks on trainee performance. |
| **Formal Evaluation** | Day 30 | Written assessment of trainee readiness. | I conduct (or designate) the Day 30 evaluation. Pass/fail determination. |

### Day 30 Evaluation — What We're Looking For

**The bar:** Can this person execute their role independently during a live shift at an acceptable standard?

**All roles:**
- Steps of service — complete, in order, without prompting
- Product knowledge — can answer guest questions, make solid recommendations
- POS proficiency — ring orders, handle modifications, close tabs without help
- Opening/closing procedures — can execute without referencing a checklist
- Time management during volume — stays composed, prioritizes correctly
- Guest interaction — confident greeting, professional engagement, genuine hospitality
- Teamwork — communicates with coworkers, supports the team, doesn't operate in a silo

**Bartender-specific:**
- Cocktail execution accuracy and consistency
- Pour consistency (jigger and free-pour)
- Bar organization and cleanliness throughout shift
- Speed benchmarks appropriate to experience level

**Server-specific:**
- Table management and rotation awareness
- Upselling execution — natural, consultative, not scripted
- Food running coordination and expo awareness
- Check average trending in the right direction

**Host-specific:**
- Seating rotation management — fair, efficient, strategic
- Waitlist communication — professional, accurate timing
- Phone etiquette and reservation system proficiency
- Guest flow management during peak hours

### Outcomes

| Result | What It Means | Trainer Payout |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **Pass** | Trainee is cleared. They're ready. | Full incentive payout. |
| **Conditional Pass** | 1-2 targeted areas need improvement. Mentor period extended 2 weeks. | Partial payout now, remainder after re-evaluation. |
| **Fail** | Trainee isn't there yet. Root cause analysis: is this a trainee issue or a training execution issue? | Payout withheld pending review. |

**The principle is simple:** We're not paying trainers for showing up to 5 shifts. We're paying them for producing a team member who can actually perform 30 days later. That's skin in the game — and it's what separates "I trained someone" from "I built a team member."

## SECTION 6: WHY THIS SCALES

This system isn't built for one location or one trainer. It's built for all three — and for whatever comes next.

- **Daily floor checklists** are role-specific, not location-specific. Venue nuances are covered in the retraining session, not the document.
- **Trainer retraining** follows the same agenda everywhere. I conduct all sessions to maintain the standard.
- **Accountability enforcement** uses the same form, same photo upload, same expectations at OAK, Cantina, and White Buffalo.
- **30-day evaluation criteria** are standardized with role-specific additions.
- **The system doesn't require me to be on-site for every training.** It requires trained trainers executing a defined process with built-in accountability. That's the difference between a program that depends on one person and a program that runs as a system.

If we open a 4th location tomorrow, we retrain the trainers, hand them the checklists, and the system runs. No reinvention. No guessing. Same standard.
