# C.O.R.E. TRAINING PROGRAM — RESTRUCTURE PROPOSAL
**Three Points Hospitality | Prepared by Devin Bostwick**
**Roles: Server, Host, Bartender | All Locations**
**Last Updated: March 15, 2026**

---

## HERE'S WHAT'S HAPPENING

The C.O.R.E. content is good. The 5-day structure works. Trainual works. Knowledge checks, mock service, the onboarding roadmap — all of that stays. None of it is the problem.

The problem is execution at the trainer level.

**What I'm seeing:**
- Accountability forms aren't getting filled out consistently — some trainers complete them, some don't, and there's no consequence either way
- End-of-shift recaps between trainer and trainee are getting skipped when it gets busy — that's exactly when they matter most
- I have zero visibility into what's actually being taught vs. what's being checked off on paper
- There's no structured touchpoint between me and the trainers during the 5-day window — trainers are essentially unsupervised once they're assigned a trainee
- The 30-day mentorship period after training? It exists on paper. That's about it.

**The result:** Some trainees come out sharp. Some need re-training within weeks. The variable isn't the program — it's the trainer. And right now, we don't have a system that holds trainers accountable for outcomes. We have a system that trusts them to figure it out. That's not working.

---

## WHAT'S NOT CHANGING

- 5-day training structure (Server, Host, Bartender)
- Trainual platform and knowledge checks
- Mock Service Examination and final evaluation
- Onboarding roadmap and pre-Day 1 requirements
- The training content itself — topics, curriculum, day-by-day progression

**The foundation is solid. We're fixing the execution layer, not rebuilding the house.**

## WHAT IS CHANGING

### 1. Three-Layer Document System

Right now we have two documents per role — a Trainer Manual and a Trainee Manual. Here's the reality: trainers aren't reading a 1,500-line document while they're behind the bar training someone during a Friday night rush. And trainees are getting a checklist that tells them *what* they learned but doesn't teach them *why* it matters when they're studying at home.

**New setup:**

| Layer | Who It's For | What It Is |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Trainer Retraining Session** | Me + each trainer | I use the current Trainer Manual as my curriculum. I physically walk each trainer through the process — I play the trainee, they demonstrate how they'd train. This is where the depth lives. One-time session, 90-120 minutes. |
| **Daily Floor Checklist** | Trainer (on shift) | One page per day. What to cover, what order, key points to hit. Checkbox format. No paragraphs, no flipping through pages. Carry it, check it off, sign it, photograph it, submit it. Done. ✅ **All 15 checklists built** (5 per role). Google Sheets builder engines built for professional printing. |
| **Enriched Trainee Manual** | Trainee (post-shift) | The study guide. Not just "today you learned about cocktail structure" — but actually explaining *what cocktail structure is* so they can review it at home after experiencing it on the floor. Reinforcement, not replacement. ✅ **All 3 role manuals built** (Bartender, Server, Host). Content protocol established. |

**Bottom line:** The trainer gets trained by me, then carries a simple checklist on the floor. The trainee gets a real study guide that makes the knowledge checks meaningful. Everybody has the right tool for the right moment.

### 2. Upgraded Accountability Form + Day 1 / Day 5 Check-Ins

I built the accountability form to give me visibility without hovering. The tool is right — the enforcement wasn't there. Here's how it changes:

**Every training shift (trainer's responsibility):**
- Enhanced accountability form — now includes: what was covered, where the trainee is struggling, and the plan for tomorrow
- Photo upload of the signed daily floor checklist
- Both submitted before the trainer leaves the building
- **No form + no photo = no trainer incentive for that shift. Non-negotiable.**

**My involvement — Day 1 and Day 5 only:**
- **Day 1:** I sit down with the trainer for 10-15 minutes. Set the tone. Make sure the trainee isn't already falling behind. Course-correct early.
- **Day 5:** Same thing. Confirm the trainee is actually ready for mock service. Get the trainer's honest read.
- **Days 2-4:** The form and checklist photo carry it. If something flags in submissions, I step in. Otherwise, the system runs.

**Why this works for my role:** I oversee multiple locations, multiple divisions, multiple trainers at once. Daily debriefs with every active trainer don't scale. But Day 1 and Day 5 are the highest-leverage touchpoints — that's where problems start and where readiness gets confirmed. The enhanced form handles the middle.

### 3. Incentive Tied to Pay

This is straightforward. The accountability form and checklist photo upload are now directly tied to the trainer's incentive. Miss the submission? Miss the pay for that shift. This isn't punitive — it's professional. We're paying you extra to train, and the documentation is part of the job. No shortcuts.

### 4. 30-Day Post-Training Mentorship — Structured

| Timeframe | What Happens |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Training Period** | 5-day C.O.R.E. program. Trainer executes daily checklist. Submits accountability form + photo. End-of-shift recaps. I check in Day 1 and Day 5. |
| **Next 30 Days** | Trainer available as a resource. Quick touchpoints on overlapping shifts. Observes trainee, notes regression or gaps. I run periodic spot-checks. |
| **Day 30** | Formal evaluation. If the trainee passes — trainer gets their full payout. |

**The principle:** Your incentive as a trainer isn't just for showing up for 5 shifts. It's for producing a team member who can actually execute 30 days later. Skin in the game.

### 5. Trainer Retraining — Before We Launch Anything

Before a single trainee enters this restructured system, I'm sitting down with every approved trainer for a 90-120 minute hands-on session. Not a meeting. Not a PowerPoint. I'm playing the trainee, and they're showing me how they train.

**What we cover:**
- Full training process walkthrough — opening procedures, closing procedures, demonstrated live
- Steps of service execution — how to teach it, not just talk about it
- Upselling strategies — when to introduce them, how to make them feel natural
- How to use the daily floor checklist
- Accountability expectations, incentive structure, 30-day mentorship
- The new standard

**Why:** "Read this manual and go train someone" is what got us here. This resets expectations by *showing* trainers what execution looks like — not telling them.
