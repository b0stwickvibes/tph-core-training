# Host Eval — Question Bank
### Three Points Hospitality Group · OAK
*30-Day Performance Evaluation · Host Role*

---

> **How This Bank Works**
> Each set contains 20 questions. During the eval, the evaluator selects **2 questions per set** (6 total across Sets A, B, C). Circle the question numbers used on the eval form. Score each on the 0–4 scale. Max per set: 8 pts. Section 2 total: 24 pts.
>
> Questions are designed to test **applied thinking**, not memorization. A host who can recite the 10-Foot Rule but can't demonstrate it under pressure hasn't learned it.

## SET A — Guest Acknowledgement & Service Standards
*"The first 15 seconds set every guest's entire experience."*

| # | Question | Answer |
|---|----------|--------|
| A-1 | How fast must a host acknowledge a guest at the stand — even when slammed? | Within **15 seconds** |
| A-2 | What is the property-wide acknowledgement standard? | Every guest acknowledged within **30 seconds** — verbal or non-verbal |
| A-3 | What counts as a valid acknowledgement when you can't stop? | Eye contact · nod · smile · *"I'll be right with you"* |
| A-4 | What is your single job in the first 15 seconds? | Set the **emotional tone** |
| A-5 | What do you do if a guest approaches within 10 feet? | Make eye contact · smile · acknowledge — every time |
| A-6 | How do you communicate wait times to avoid confusion? | Be **specific** — *"about 10 minutes"*, not *"shouldn't be long"* |
| A-7 | If a wait is running longer than quoted, when do you update the guest? | **Before** the deadline passes — proactively, not when they ask |
| A-8 | What is the host stand posture standard? | **Standing** — no leaning, no slouching |
| A-9 | What is the phone standard at the host stand during service? | Phone is **not visible** |
| A-10 | Is the host role primarily gatekeeper or service? | **Service** — always |
| A-11 | Seating decisions are made for whose benefit? | The **guest** — never staff convenience |
| A-12 | Name one "over-the-top" host move that costs nothing. | Use guest name · flag a celebration · offer a drink during the wait |
| A-13 | What does "Team for Customer Service" mean in practice? | **Everyone owns every guest** — no *"not my table"* |
| A-14 | How do you handle a service recovery situation? | **Acknowledge → Apologize → Act → Exceed** |
| A-15 | Recovery must begin within how long of identifying the issue? | **5 minutes** |
| A-16 | What should guests never feel during a rush? | The **pressure** of a full dining room |
| A-17 | What should you know before service starts to answer guest questions confidently? | Daily specials + basic menu and bar info |
| A-18 | What's the standard if your greeting sounds scripted or robotic? | Make it a **welcome**, not a transaction — warmth is non-negotiable |
| A-19 | What's the minimum acceptable greeting when you're genuinely overwhelmed? | Acknowledge + set expectation: *"Hi — I'll be right with you"* |
| A-20 | What proves you're managing expectations instead of guessing? | Specific timeline + **proactive updates** before the guest has to ask |

## SET B — Resy, Reservations & Waitlist Management
*"A quote is a promise. Silence is a failure."*

| # | Question | Answer |
|---|----------|--------|
| B-1 | What is the rule on quoted wait times? | A quote is a **promise** — not an estimate |
| B-2 | If the actual wait exceeds your quote by more than 10 minutes, what do you do? | **Notify manager** + proactively update the guest immediately |
| B-3 | What is the single worst move when a wait is running long? | **Silence** |
| B-4 | What should you review in Resy before service begins? | Full reservation list + **all guest notes** |
| B-5 | Reservation notes must be read — and then what? | **Actioned** — reading them is not enough |
| B-6 | Name 4 Resy table statuses. | Available · Seated · Cleaning · On Hold |
| B-7 | What is the non-negotiable Resy standard during service? | Floor map **accurate in real time** — always |
| B-8 | What does an inaccurate floor map directly cause? | Double-seating · host errors · server overload |
| B-9 | Waitlist management includes which 3 core actions? | Add walk-ins · quote the wait · notify when ready |
| B-10 | What should wait quotes be based on? | **Resy data + floor observation** — not a gut feeling |
| B-11 | How do you respond when a guest says the wait is too long? | Acknowledge + offer alternatives + give a **specific updated time** |
| B-12 | Name 3 alternatives you can offer during a long wait. | Bar seating · outdoor seating (if available) · complimentary drink during wait |
| B-13 | What should you never do when quoting a wait? | Promise **shorter** than your data supports |
| B-14 | A guest says they have a reservation and claims they've been waiting — what's step one? | Pull them up in **Resy immediately** |
| B-15 | A reservation party has waited 10+ minutes past their time — what happens next? | **Get a manager** |
| B-16 | What should you avoid saying during a reservation mix-up? | Blame the system · make excuses · say *"I don't know"* without a follow-up action |
| B-17 | What is the fastest way to create a service failure before guests ever sit down? | Missing an **allergy note or celebration note** in Resy |
| B-18 | When should special occasion info be added or confirmed in Resy? | **Before** the server has to ask — proactively at or before seating |
| B-19 | What is the real cost of under-quoting a wait? | Lost **trust** + negative reviews + guest won't return |
| B-20 | What does trainer-validated Resy competency look like? | Live or simulated: seat a waitlist party + **update table status accurately** in real time |

## SET C — Seating, Rotation & Floor Awareness
*"The host who doesn't watch the floor owns every problem that comes from it."*

| # | Question | Answer |
|---|----------|--------|
| C-1 | Who owns section balance during service? | **Host + manager** — shared responsibility |
| C-2 | How often should you scan the floor map or physically scan the room? | Every **10 minutes** minimum |
| C-3 | How do you decide which section to seat a new party in? | Check section balance + server load — seat to avoid overloading any one section. |
| C-4 | What do you do if a server is overloaded during service? | Redirect seating to another section and notify the manager. |
| C-5 | How do you announce a newly seated party to the server? | Notify the server with party size + any notes (no silent seats). |
| C-6 | What’s the standard for pacing seats during peak service? | Pace seating to prevent server/kitchen overload; adjust as needed with manager direction. |
| C-7 | If your scan reveals a problem, what is the rule? | **Act immediately** — do not wait to see if it resolves |
| C-8 | What is the most common host error that damages the entire room? | **Overseating one section** |
| C-9 | When can you honor a guest's table change request? | If the table is available **and** it doesn't disrupt floor balance |
| C-10 | If the preferred table isn't available, what do you offer? | A **priority wait** with a specific time — not a vague "maybe" |
| C-11 | Can you seat above a server's section max during peak? | Only with **manager authorization** |
| C-12 | After seating a table, what is your responsibility to the service team? | **Announce the guest to their server** — no silent seats |
| C-13 | What must be true about a table before you seat guests at it? | **Clean · stable · properly set** — no exceptions |
| C-14 | What is your seating pacing job when the restaurant is slammed? | Pace seats so **servers and the kitchen don't crash** — protect the whole room |
| C-15 | If one server is clearly overloaded, how should your seating decisions change? | **Redirect** new seats to other sections until balance is restored |
| C-16 | Name one observable sign that a table is "stuck" and may need intervention. | Plates sitting too long · visibly waiting for a check · no server contact in 10+ minutes |
| C-17 | What is one simple way to elevate the guest experience while escorting to a table? | **Confident lead + light, genuine conversation** — don't just walk them silently |
| C-18 | A reserved party hasn't arrived within 10 minutes of their reservation time — what do you do? | **Alert the manager** |
| C-19 | If a party on the waitlist needs attention while you're tied up, who do you loop in? | **The bar** — offer the connection proactively |
| C-20 | Seating decisions are made for whose benefit — always? | The **guest** — never staff convenience |

## Evaluator Notes — Using This Bank

### Scoring Reminder
| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **4** | Answered correctly, confidently, without hesitation |
| **3** | Correct answer, minor gaps in language or delivery |
| **2** | Partial answer — got the concept but missed key detail |
| **1** | Significant gaps — needed prompting to get close |
| **0** | Could not answer or answered incorrectly |

### What "Correct" Actually Means for a Host
A host role is about **execution under pressure** — not recitation. When scoring, weight confidence and real-world framing. Questions now emphasize "how" and "what do you do" to test applied thinking:

- ❌ *"The 10-Foot Rule is when you're 10 feet away..."* — reciting a definition
- ✅ *"If I'm within 10 feet of anyone — guest, waiting party, anyone — I make eye contact and acknowledge them. No exceptions."* — owns it

### Red Flags to Note
- Hesitation on A-1, A-2, A-4 — these are the core non-negotiables
- Any answer that references waiting for a manager **before** acknowledging the guest
- Inability to name Resy statuses or describe the floor scan process
- "I would just tell them..." language without a specific recovery action
- Vague responses to scenario-based questions (e.g., "I'd handle it" instead of specific steps)
