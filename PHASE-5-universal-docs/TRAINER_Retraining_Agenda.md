## PURPOSE

This agenda runs the trainer retraining workshop before any new C.O.R.E. training cohort launches. Every trainer — regardless of role or location — attends this session. Role-specific inserts are loaded into Block 3.

## BLOCK 1: WHY THE SYSTEM CHANGED (45 min)

**Facilitator note:** Do not rush this block. Trainers need to understand the problem before they will respect the solution. Keep it honest — the old system had real gaps.

### The Problem With the Old System (10 min)
- Training was inconsistent across locations and trainers
- Trainers had no daily reference tool — they were improvising
- No way to verify what actually happened on a training shift
- Trainee quality was trainer-dependent, not system-dependent

### What C.O.R.E. Fixes (15 min)
- C.O.R.E. = Consistent, Operational, Repeatable, Excellence
- Three-layer system: Floor Checklist + Enriched Trainee Manual + 30-Day Evaluation
- Every training shift is documented, signed, and submitted
- Trainer accountability is built in — not assumed

### What This Means for You (20 min)
- Your job is not to teach everything — it is to demonstrate, coach, and verify
- The checklist tells you what to cover. The trainee manual reinforces it after the shift. Your job is the floor.
- Trainer incentive is tied directly to system compliance: checklist submitted + accountability form filed + 30-day evaluation completed

> **Discussion prompt:** "What is the most common thing new hires do not know by their first solo shift? Why does that keep happening?"

## BLOCK 2: THE THREE-LAYER SYSTEM (60 min)

### Layer 1: The Daily Floor Checklist (20 min)
- One checklist per training day per role
- The trainer carries it, checks off each topic as it is covered on the floor
- Key points under each checkbox = reminders, not teaching scripts
- End-of-Shift Recap is non-negotiable — 10 minutes before the trainee clocks out
- Both trainer and trainee sign. Trainer photographs and submits.

**Walk through the locked format together:**
- Header fields (date, location, trainer, trainee)
- Topic checkboxes with key points
- End-of-Shift Recap block (CFU question, scenario, tomorrow's preview)
- Submission confirmation checkboxes
- Dual signature block

> **Key rule:** If it is not on the checklist, it is not part of that day's training. Do not freelance.

### Layer 2: The Enriched Trainee Manual (20 min)
- Lives in Trainual — the trainee reviews it after each shift
- Not a teaching document: it reinforces what the trainee already practiced
- Organized by day and category, matching the checklist exactly
- The trainee's Knowledge Check at the end of each day comes from this material
- Your job: remind the trainee to complete their Trainual review before the next shift

**How to verify completion before starting the next day:**
- Open Trainual on your phone or tablet before the shift begins
- Pull up your trainee's profile and check the progress indicator for the previous day's topic
- Green = complete. Anything else = not complete — follow the missed Knowledge Check protocol before proceeding
- Do not assume completion. Check it. If you start Day 2 without confirming Day 1 is green, the layer system breaks.

> **Key rule:** A trainer who does not check Trainual is a trainer running on assumptions. That is how gaps stay invisible.

### Layer 3: The 30-Day Evaluation (20 min)
- Conducted 30 days after the trainee's first solo shift
- Covers: service standard execution, product knowledge, teamwork, guest interaction
- Scored as Pass, Conditional Pass, or Needs Improvement
- Trainer completes the evaluation alongside the GM
- Outcome determines: continued solo shifts, coaching plan, or re-training
- This is where the 30-day trainer bonus is earned

> **The checklist is a coverage guarantee — not a script and not a lecture outline.** Your job is to demonstrate and coach during live service. The checklist ensures nothing gets skipped. It does not tell you how to talk.

### INSERT A: BARTENDER TRAINERS

**Day 1 — Orientation & Bar Foundations (15 min)**
- Focus areas: venue orientation, bar layout, standard setup, spirit knowledge foundations
- Common trainer gap: skipping the "why" behind setup sequence — cover this explicitly
- CFU to practice: "Walk me through your bar setup sequence. What goes wrong if you start with glassware instead of ice?"

**Day 2 — Cocktail Foundations & Technical Skills (15 min)**
- Focus areas: pouring technique, build sequence, POS navigation
- Common trainer gap: letting trainees almost hit the pour spec — hold the standard
- CFU to practice: "If a 4-count free pour hits 1.5oz, what happens to cost if you are consistently at 1.75?"

**Day 3 — High-Volume Service, Upselling & Cross-Training (15 min)**
- Focus areas: speed without sacrificing accuracy, upsell language, expo cross-training
- Common trainer gap: skipping upsell coaching because it feels like a sales exercise — it is a service standard
- CFU to practice: "Give me your upsell line for a well cocktail upgrade."

**Day 4 — Alcohol Service, Garnishing & Drink Presentation (15 min)**
- Focus areas: responsible service signs, garnish execution, closing systems
- Common trainer gap: treating responsible service as a lecture instead of a judgment call exercise
- Scenario to practice: "A guest orders their fourth drink in 90 minutes and is starting to slur. What do you do?"

**Day 5 — Prove It (15 min)**
- Assessment day — trainer observes and scores; do not coach during the evaluation
- Use the 30-Day Eval Rubric format to run Day 5's informal benchmark

### INSERT B: SERVER TRAINERS

**Day 1 — Orientation & Service Foundations (15 min)**
- Focus areas: venue layout, table numbering, steps of service, POS basics
- Common trainer gap: assuming the trainee knows what "steps of service" means — define it explicitly
- CFU to practice: "Walk me through a table from greeting to payment. Hit every required touchpoint."

**Day 2 — Strengthening Service Skills (15 min)**
- Focus areas: order accuracy, modifier entry, side work, section management
- Common trainer gap: taking over when the trainee struggles instead of coaching through it
- CFU to practice: "A guest changes their order after you have already put it in. Walk me through how you handle it in POS."

**Day 3 — Menu Knowledge & Complex Service Scenarios (15 min)**
- Focus areas: food and beverage descriptions, allergy awareness, handling complaints
- Common trainer gap: letting vague menu descriptions slide — hold the 8-12 word standard
- CFU to practice: "Describe our top-selling entree in 10 words or fewer. No feeling words."

**Day 4 — Guest Relations & Closing Procedures (15 min)**
- Focus areas: table turns, check presentation, side work completion, closing duties
- Common trainer gap: skipping closing procedures because the shift ran long
- Scenario to practice: "A guest receives the wrong entree. You did not take the order. What do you do?"

**Day 5 — Prove It (15 min)**
- Assessment day — trainer observes, does not intervene
- Score against: greeting timing, order accuracy, upsell attempt, check delivery, side work completion

### INSERT C: HOST TRAINERS

**Day 1 — Orientation & Host Foundations (15 min)**
- Focus areas: floor layout, seating sections, reservation platform, greeting standard
- Common trainer gap: not enforcing the 60-second greeting window — it is a standard, not a suggestion
- CFU to practice: "What is the first thing you say when a guest walks in? Give me the exact line."

**Day 2 — Hosting Fundamentals & Reservation Mastery (15 min)**
- Focus areas: reservation system navigation, waitlist management, quote accuracy
- Common trainer gap: teaching the system clicks without teaching the communication behind them
- CFU to practice: "A guest without a reservation asks for a table for 4 on a 45-minute wait. What do you say?"

**Day 3 — Guest Experience & High-Volume Hosting (15 min)**
- Focus areas: pacing large parties, managing walk-in pressure, communication with servers
- Common trainer gap: letting the trainee hide behind the podium instead of working the floor
- Scenario to practice: "Three tables just walked in. Two have reservations. One does not. Walk me through your decision."

**Day 4 — Team Integration & Multitask Mastery (15 min)**
- Focus areas: server communication, section rotation, phone handling, resolving seating conflicts
- Common trainer gap: not practicing the phone greeting out loud — trainees guess at it
- CFU to practice: "What is the phone greeting? Give it to me."

**Day 5 — Prove It (15 min)**
- Assessment day — trainer observes, does not intervene
- Score against: greeting timing, reservation accuracy, seating flow, team communication, problem handling

## BLOCK 4: ACCOUNTABILITY + INCENTIVE STRUCTURE (45 min)

### The Accountability Form (15 min)
- Submitted by the trainee at the end of every training shift via the app
- Three questions confirmed live — see `ACCOUNTABILITY_Form_Spec.md` for exact questions
- Photo upload of the signed checklist is required
- No submission = incomplete training shift — that shift does not count toward the trainer incentive

> **Walk through the form live.** Every trainer submits a test entry before leaving this room.

### The Incentive Structure (15 min)

*Confirm exact dollar amounts with Tony before this section.*

| Incentive | Trigger | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-Shift Bonus** | Checklist submitted + accountability form filed same day | $TBD |
| **Completion Bonus** | Trainee completes all 5 days with no missed submissions | $TBD |
| **30-Day Bonus** | 30-day evaluation completed + trainee still on schedule | $TBD |

**Sizing guidance for Tony conversation:** The per-shift bonus should be enough to offset the paperwork friction — it keeps trainers compliant day-to-day. The 30-day bonus should be the largest of the three — it's the only incentive tied to actual trainee retention and long-term performance. If the 30-day bonus is smaller than the completion bonus, trainers are incentivized to finish the week, not to care what happens after it.

**Hard rules:**
- Incentives are not retroactive — missed submissions cannot be made up
- If a trainee no-shows, the trainer receives regular hourly for that shift only
- Disputes go to the GM, not the training lead

### Documentation Standards (15 min)
- Signed checklist photograph = primary paper trail
- Accountability form = digital record
- Both are required for every shift — not one or the other
- GM audits weekly. Patterns of incomplete submissions affect trainer eligibility for future cohorts.

## TRAINER CONDUCT STANDARDS

These apply on every training shift, regardless of how busy it gets. Non-negotiable.

- **Do not run their tables for them.** If a trainee is struggling, coach through it — do not take over. Taking over protects the shift and breaks the training.
- **Do not skip the recap.** Busy service does not cancel the recap. It gets shorter if needed, but it happens every shift without exception.
- **Do not give answers during a check-for-understanding.** Redirect. Ask again. Wait. Giving the answer tells you nothing about what the trainee actually retained.
- **Do not freelance the curriculum.** If it is not on the checklist, it is not part of today's training. Add-ons create inconsistency across trainers and locations.
- **Do not let a trainee submit the accountability form for them.** The trainee submits it. The trainer confirms it was submitted before leaving the building. Those are two different actions.

## ESCALATION PROTOCOL

Not everything stays between trainer and trainee. These situations go to the GM immediately.

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Trainee is combative or refusing direction | Stop the training shift. GM takes over. |
| Trainee no-shows without contact | Log it. GM determines whether to continue the cohort. |
| Missed accountability form two shifts in a row | Flag to GM. Do not continue to Day 3 without resolution. |
| Trainee fails a CFU twice on the same topic | Note it on the checklist. Flag to GM before Day 3. |
| Trainer suspects a trainee is impaired | Remove from floor immediately. GM handles. |
| Documentation dispute (trainer vs. trainee account) | Do not resolve it yourself. GM mediates with the signed checklist as the record. |

**The rule:** If you are unsure whether something is your call, it is not your call. Escalate.

## COMMON TRAINING FAILURES

These are the most frequent ways the system breaks down. Know them before you hit the floor.

**Trainer-side failures:**
- Skipping the End-of-Shift Recap because service ran long
- Doing the trainee's job during service instead of letting them work through it
- Signing the checklist without actually covering every item
- Assuming Trainual completion without checking the profile
- Coaching during Day 5 evaluation instead of observing

**Trainee-side failures (trainer is responsible for catching these):**
- Submitting the accountability form without completing the Knowledge Check
- Completing side work only when watched
- Giving correct answers during the recap but not executing on the floor
- Avoiding the GM or trainer after a mistake instead of reporting it

**System failures (escalate to GM):**
- Accountability form submissions not matching checklist dates
- Trainee advancing to the next day without the previous day's checklist signed
- 30-day evaluation not triggered within the required window after first solo shift

## BLOCK 5: LIVE DEMO + SCENARIO PRACTICE (45 min)

Trainers practice the trainer experience, not the trainee experience. They need to know what it feels like to run a check-for-understanding and hold a standard they did not set.

### Exercise 1: The Recap (15 min)
Pair trainers up. One plays trainer, one plays trainee.
- Trainer runs the End-of-Shift Recap from their role's Day 1 checklist
- Trainee gives an intentionally weak answer
- Trainer must redirect without giving the answer outright
- Switch roles. Debrief.

### Exercise 2: The Standard Hold (15 min)
Facilitator presents three scenarios. Trainers call out what they would do.

> **Scenario 1:** Your trainee is consistently over-pouring by half a count. They are fast and guests like them. Do you say something?
> *Answer: Yes. The standard is the standard. Speed and personality do not override pour spec.*

> **Scenario 2:** Your trainee skipped an upsell on three tables in a row. They are otherwise solid. Do you address it?
> *Answer: Yes, during the recap — not in front of guests.*

> **Scenario 3:** Your trainee forgot to complete their Trainual review and did not do the Knowledge Check. Their shift starts in 10 minutes. What do you do?
> *Answer: The trainee completes the missed Knowledge Check during their pre-shift meal or first break — before the day's training content begins. You do not sign off on the Day 2 checklist until the Day 1 Trainual content shows green in their profile. The shift runs, but the sign-off does not happen until the gap is closed. Flag to GM if it becomes a pattern.*

### Exercise 3: Checklist Run-Through (15 min)
Each trainer picks one day from their role's checklist and walks the group through it as if orienting a trainee before the shift starts.
- Time them: should take no more than 2 minutes
- Debrief: what felt awkward, what was skipped, what needs to be said differently
