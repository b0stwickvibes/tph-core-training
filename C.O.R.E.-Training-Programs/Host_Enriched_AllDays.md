## Customer Interaction & Experience `[Guest-Facing]`

**You are the first and last face every guest sees. That matters more than any other role.**

- **The Approach:** Warm, immediate, eye contact, smile. "Welcome to [venue name]". every single time. Waitlist quotes are honest. Phone calls answered within 3 rings with the standard greeting.
- **Energy:** Genuine and present. A great host doesn't just seat people. they set the tone for the entire experience.
- **Scenarios:** A guest walks in frustrated about a long wait. Acknowledge the frustration, give an honest time estimate, and offer alternatives (bar seating, patio). Empathy first, logistics second.
- **Pitfalls:** Ignoring a guest at the door because you're on the phone. A quick "Welcome. I'll be right with you" takes two seconds and changes everything.

## Event & Reservation Management `[Process]`

**Large party reservations, event inquiries, and VIP recognition.**

- **Audit:** Large party procedure (info to collect, confirmation calls). Event inquiries logged and directed. No-show protocol (hold time, release, documentation). VIP/regular flagging in the system.

## Reservation Expertise (Resy) `[Skill]`

**Create, adjust, and cancel bookings in Resy. keep the system updated in real time.**

- **Spec:** Create reservations with full details (name, phone, party size, time, special notes). Block time slots for large parties. Prevent overbooking by monitoring availability before confirming.
- **Metrics:** Every reservation accurate and complete. System updated within 60 seconds of any change.
- **Pro Tip:** Details matter. always repeat guest names and reservation times to prevent miscommunication.
- **Misses:** Leaving old reservations in the system or failing to update cancellations. A stale Resy board creates confusion for the entire floor.

---

## Answering Calls & Making Reservations `[Guest-Facing]`

**Answer calls with the standard greeting. professional, warm, and efficient.**

- **The Approach:** Answer within 3 rings. Gather: guest name, phone number, email, party size, special requests. Confirm all details back before ending the call.
- **Energy:** Smile when you talk. it makes a difference in tone. Phone guests deserve the same warmth as walk-ins.
- **Scenarios:** A guest calls during a fully booked time slot. Offer the next available time, add them to the waitlist, or suggest an alternative date. Never just say "we're full."
- **Pitfalls:** Rushing through a phone call because walk-ins are waiting. Neither guest should feel like an afterthought.

---

## Technology Utilization (iPad/Resy) `[Process]`

**Use Notes, Floor View, Waitlist, and Reservation tools inside Resy efficiently.**

Keep the digital system updated in real time during service. The iPad is your control panel. Floor View for table status, Waitlist for tracking walk-ins, Notes for special requests and guest preferences. If the system is outdated, the floor is flying blind.

---

## Phone Etiquette & Guest Communication `[Guest-Facing]`

**Handle incoming calls during live service while greeting walk-in guests simultaneously.**

Balance phone professionalism with in-person warmth. Neither guest should feel rushed. If you're mid-call when a walk-in arrives, make eye contact and acknowledge them. If you're mid-greeting when the phone rings, the walk-in takes priority. the phone can ring one more time.

## Seating Guests `[Guest-Facing]`

- **Scenarios:** A walk-in couple during a wait asks "How long?" Give an honest answer. If it's 20 minutes, say 20. Underselling the wait creates more frustration than the wait itself.

## Special Requests, Waitlists & Guest Concerns `[Guest-Facing]`

**Work through real scenarios: dietary requests, unexpected large parties, guest dissatisfaction, waitlist management.**

- **The Approach:** Quote accurate wait times (honest, not optimistic). Communicate clearly when it's longer than expected. Keep walk-in guests engaged while they wait. Dietary requests and special accommodations get communicated to the server and kitchen immediately.
- **Energy:** Poised and positive. Guests remember how you made them feel, even in tough moments.
- **Scenarios:** A walk-in party of 6 arrives during a fully booked hour. Quote the real wait time, offer bar seating while they wait, and add them to the waitlist. Don't promise what you can't deliver.
- **Pitfalls:** Telling a guest "just a few more minutes" when it's going to be 20. Inaccurate wait quotes erode trust faster than a long wait does.

---

## Problem Resolution & Rebuttals `[Guest-Facing]`

**Practice responding to complaints calmly and quickly. De-escalation is your best tool.**

- **The Approach:** Acknowledge the concern, offer a solution, and follow through. Review common rebuttal scripts from the Host Handbook. Practice declining requests gracefully and redirecting guests professionally.
- **Energy:** Empathetic and firm. You're solving the problem, not winning an argument.
- **Scenarios:** A guest is upset about their seating arrangement. Listen, acknowledge, and offer an alternative. If no alternative exists, explain honestly and involve the MOD if needed.
- **Pitfalls:** Getting defensive or dismissive. "I understand your frustration" goes further than "That's our policy." Empathy and follow-up turn negative experiences around.
