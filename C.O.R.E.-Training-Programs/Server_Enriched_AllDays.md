---

## Step 1: Welcome to the Server Training Program

Welcome to the Server C.O.R.E. Training Program for Three Points Hospitality. Over the next five days, you'll build the skills and knowledge to run a section at any TPH venue. This program is your reference for every training shift and every topic you'll be tested on.

---

## Step 2: Purpose & Timeline

📌 **The Rules:**

- You have **10 days** from your first training shift to pass your test.
- You can test as early as Day 1 if you're ready.
- You must demonstrate you can perform server duties efficiently alongside your team.
- **Until you pass: no regular schedule, no tips.**

This program covers everything on the test. Use it as your study guide, shift reference, and prep tool from Day 1 through test day.

---

## Step 3: Targets

**When you complete this training, you will be able to:**

- Take and deliver orders accurately and efficiently.
- Provide exceptional customer service, ensuring guest satisfaction.
- Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of the menu, including ingredients and preparation methods.
- Handle payments and operate the POS system proficiently.
- Maintain a clean and organized dining area.

---

## Step 4: Required Testing

**You will be tested on:**

- 📖 Server Manual
- 🛡️ Responsible Alcohol Service

## Customer Interaction & Experience `[Guest-Facing]`

**First impressions: how to greet a table (timing, body language, eye contact, energy).**

- **The Approach:** Read the guest. business dinner vs. date night vs. birthday vs. casual. Adjust your energy and pacing accordingly.
- **Energy:** Warm, confident, and genuine. Every table gets a strong first impression.
- **Scenarios:** A guest is on the phone when you approach. Wait, make eye contact, and return in 30 seconds. Don't hover.
- **Pitfalls:** Using the same scripted greeting for every table. Guests can tell when you're on autopilot.

---

## Clearing Tables & Service Etiquette `[Process]`

**When to clear, how to clear, and how to keep a table clean throughout the meal.**

- **Objective:** Read the table. don't rush, don't let plates stack up. Clear from the right side. Never scrape plates in front of guests.
- **Audit:** Crumbing between courses when applicable. Pre-bussing throughout the meal, not just at the end.
- **Reset:** Reset for the next course or the next guest. Speed and cleanliness matter equally.
- **Result:** The table stays clean and presentable from first course to farewell.

## Menu Training `[Skill]`

**Review key menu items: ingredients, preparation methods, guest favorites, seasonal specials.**

- **Spec:** Know high-margin items and how to describe them confidently. Understand preparation methods so you can answer "How is that made?" without hesitation.
- **Metrics:** Describe 5 menu items accurately. ingredients, preparation, and what makes each one a standout.
- **Pro Tip:** A deep understanding of the menu makes you a trusted resource for guests. Confidence in descriptions drives guest trust and upsell success.
- **Misses:** Describing a dish vaguely ("It's really good") instead of specifically ("It's pan-seared with a lemon butter sauce, served over roasted vegetables").

---

## Review of Food Allergies `[Skill]`

**Common allergens: nuts, dairy, gluten, shellfish, soy, eggs.**

- **Spec:** Know the severity of allergic reactions and cross-contamination prevention. A food allergy is a safety issue, not a preference.
- **Metrics:** Identify which menu items contain each major allergen. Know the protocol when a guest discloses an allergy.
- **Pro Tip:** When in doubt, always check with the kitchen. Over-communicating an allergy is better than under-communicating one.
- **Misses:** Assuming a dish is safe because "it probably doesn't have that." Never guess on allergens. Ever.

---

## Review of Dietary Options `[Process]`

**Dietary restrictions: vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, keto.**

Know which menu items cater to each and what substitutions are available. Being prepared with answers makes service smoother. guests shouldn't have to wait while you figure it out.

---

## Beverage Service Basics `[Skill]`

**Overview of full bar offerings: liquor, beer, wine, signature cocktails, non-alcoholic options.**

- **Spec:** Age verification procedures and responsible alcohol service. Know what's on tap, what's by the glass, and what pairs well with food.
- **Metrics:** Suggest at least one drink pairing per entrée category.
- **Pro Tip:** A confident drink recommendation elevates the meal. "The Malbec pairs beautifully with the steak" sounds better than "We have red wine."
- **Misses:** Serving alcohol without checking ID when required. Responsible service is non-negotiable.
