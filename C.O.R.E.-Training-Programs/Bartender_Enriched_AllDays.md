## Spirit Categories Overview `[Skill]`

**Walk through the 6 spirit categories: Vodka, Gin, Rum, Tequila/Mezcal, Whiskey, Brandy/Cognac.**

- **Spec:** Six categories. Each has a distinct base ingredient, flavor profile, and role in cocktail creation. You need to name all six and explain how each shows up in guest orders.
- **Metrics:** Point out where each category lives on the back bar and well. Identify at least 2 brands per category at your venue by end of Day 1.
- **Pro Tip:** Tie spirits to cocktails. "Our Reposado makes an incredible Margarita, smooth with a hint of oak" beats "We have great tequila."
- **Misses:** Blanking on recommendations. If you don't know yet, say "Let me find the best option for you." Never guess, never shrug.

## Product Knowledge: Flavor Profiles & Brands `[Skill]`

**Walk the back bar and identify every bottle by name, category, and price tier.**

- **Spec:** Know the difference between well, call, premium, and top shelf. Understand what makes a bourbon different from a rye, a reposado from a blanco, a London Dry from a New Western gin.
- **Metrics:** Identify house pours and top 5 most-ordered spirits at your venue. Reference your Product List (Welcome Packet → Important Information).
- **Pro Tip:** "That's a great choice. If you like that, you'd also love [X]." That one sentence elevates the experience and the check.
- **Misses:** Treating all bottles as interchangeable. A guest ordering Patrón doesn't want to hear "tequila is tequila." Know the tiers, know the differences.

## Introduction to Bartending `[Guest-Facing]`

**Brief history of bartending, professional expectations, and responsibilities.**

Bartending is creating an experience. Every drink, every conversation, every handoff is your reputation. Core duties: drink preparation, guest interaction, bar maintenance, cash handling, opening/closing. The best bartenders treat it like a craft, and that mindset shows in every interaction.

---

## SCS & The Basics `[Guest-Facing]`

**Introduction to Service, Consistency, and Speed (SCS): the backbone of every guest interaction.**

- **The Approach:** SCS is the step-by-step framework: Greet within 30 seconds → Identify the occasion → Suggest with confidence → Deliver with care → Check back → Close strong.
- **Energy:** Consistently great. A great bartender isn't just fast; they're fast *and* consistent.
- **Scenarios:** A couple sits down. You ask "What brings you in tonight, celebrating anything?" That one question changes the entire interaction and opens the door to premium suggestions.
- **Pitfalls:** Skipping steps when it gets busy. SCS works because it's repeatable. Every guest, every time. No shortcuts.

---

## Building Cocktails & Basic Mixing `[Skill]`

**Introduction to cocktail structure: base spirit, modifier, sweetener, acid, garnish.**

- **Spec:** Every cocktail follows the same formula: Base Spirit + Modifier + Sweetener + Acid + Garnish. Shaken (8–10 sec) = citrus/juice. Stirred (20–30 sec) = spirit-forward. No exceptions.
- **Metrics:** Identify the structure in at least 3 classic cocktails (e.g., Margarita = tequila + triple sec + agave + lime + salt rim). Understand the role of ice in dilution and temperature.
- **Pro Tip:** When you hear the ice "snap" in the shaker, you're close to proper dilution. Consistent dilution = consistent drinks.
- **Misses:** Shaking a Martini (cloudy, over-diluted). Stirring a Margarita (won't integrate citrus). Always match technique to ingredients.

# Mixing Technique

> **What This Covers:** Precision pouring, tool handling, and the physical mechanics of building cocktails (shaking, stirring, and measuring).

---

## Jigger Types & Proper Pouring Techniques `[Skill]`

**Learn different jigger sizes and techniques for accurate pouring.**

- **Spec:** Jigger sizes: 0.5oz, 0.75oz, 1oz, 1.5oz, 2oz. Fill to the top of the rim, not below, not overflowing.
- **Metrics:** Every pour within spec. No eyeballing. Consistency starts with the jigger.
- **Pro Tip:** Hold the jigger between your index and middle finger at the waist for maximum control and speed.
- **Misses:** Rushing the pour and spilling over. A sloppy jigger pour is no better than a bad free pour.

---

## Measuring Techniques `[Skill]`

**Understand under-pouring vs. over-pouring and what each costs.**

Under-pouring kills the drink. Over-pouring kills the margins. Every ounce matters. precision protects quality and cost control at the same time.

---

## Building a Cocktail `[Skill]`

**Walk through cocktail structure: base spirit, modifier, sweetener, acid, garnish.**

- **Spec:** Every cocktail follows a formula. Build one component by component, understanding why each ingredient is there.
- **Metrics:** Identify the role of each component in the drink. Know what changes if you add too much or too little of any element.
- **Pro Tip:** Taste matters more than technique at this stage. If you understand *why* a drink works, you'll build it better every time.
- **Misses:** Dumping ingredients without understanding proportions. Structure first, speed later.

---

## Hands-On Tool Familiarization `[Skill]`

**Handle every tool: shaker tin, mixing glass, bar spoon, muddler, strainers, peeler, pour spouts.**

- **Spec:** Pick up each tool, use it, put it back. Practice the motions: shake, stir, strain, pour.
- **Metrics:** Grab the right tool without looking. Execute the motion cleanly.
- **Pro Tip:** Everything has a home. Put it back. Build the habit now. Mid-rush is not the time to learn where things go.
- **Misses:** Handling tools loosely or incorrectly. Grip matters for control and speed.

---

## Introduction to Shaking Techniques `[Skill]`

**When to shake: citrus, cream, egg white, anything that needs aeration.**

- **Spec:** Proper grip, controlled motion, 8–10 seconds standard. Shake with purpose. The goal is aeration and dilution, not a workout.
- **Metrics:** Consistent dilution on every shake. Practice with ice and water until the timing is automatic.
- **Pro Tip:** Listen for the ice. When it changes from rattling to a smooth shake, you're close to proper dilution.
- **Misses:** Over-shaking (watered down) or under-shaking (too strong, poorly integrated). Hit the window every time.

---

## Introduction to Stirring Techniques `[Skill]`

**When to stir: spirit-forward cocktails (Manhattan, Negroni, Old Fashioned).**

- **Spec:** Smooth bar spoon rotation, minimal splash, 20–30 seconds. Stirring preserves the drink's clarity and texture.
- **Metrics:** Consistent dilution without over-watering. The drink should be cold and silky, not thin.
- **Pro Tip:** Keep the spoon against the glass wall. Smooth rotation = clean dilution. Choppy stirring = uneven drink.
- **Misses:** Stirring too aggressively (introduces air, clouds the drink) or too short (under-diluted, harsh).

---

## Pouring Orientation `[Skill]`

**Introduction to free pouring, pour counts, and when to use a jigger vs. free pour.**

- **Spec:** Standard count: 4-count = 1.5oz (venue standard). Free pouring is a skill earned through accuracy, not speed.
- **Metrics:** Consistent 4-count pours that match jigger verification.
- **Pro Tip:** Speed is earned. Accuracy comes first. Use the jigger until cleared for free pour.
- **Misses:** Free pouring before you're accurate. Over-pouring kills margins. Under-pouring kills the drink.

---

## Bottle Holding Techniques `[Skill]`

**Single bottle hold: grip, angle, pour control. Multi-bottle hold: managing two bottles in one hand.**

Practice both in free-pour and jigger-pour scenarios. Clean bottle handling reduces strain and increases speed during high-volume service.

---

## Free-Pour Practice `[Skill]`

**Set up test bottles with water. practice pour counts, check accuracy with jigger after each pour.**

- **Spec:** Pour, measure, adjust. If you're consistently over or under, recalibrate your count. This is a 30-minute end-of-shift drill.
- **Metrics:** Pours within ±0.25oz of target consistently. Track improvement across sessions.
- **Pro Tip:** Slow your count down if you're over-pouring. Speed up if you're under. Small adjustments make the difference.
- **Misses:** Practicing without measuring after. If you don't check, you don't improve.

## Customer Service & Guest Engagement `[Guest-Facing]`

**Key service etiquette: attentiveness, body language, tone, pacing.**

- **The Approach:** 5-10 rule, eye contact, confident welcoming language.
- **Energy:** Warm, professional, and present. You're not just making drinks. You're hosting.
- **Scenarios:** A guest sits down and says nothing. Acknowledge within 30 seconds, make eye contact, let them know you're there. That first moment sets the tone.
- **Pitfalls:** Ignoring a guest because you're mid-build. A quick "I'll be right with you" takes two seconds and changes everything.

---

## Upselling: Fundamentals & Product Knowledge `[Guest-Facing]`

**Ethical upselling: suggest the upgrade, don't push the sale.**

- **The Approach:** Enhance the experience, not the check. Review premium products and practice assessing guest preferences. Recommend based on what they describe, not what you want to sell.
- **Energy:** Confident and natural. Knowledge is not power. Actionable and executed knowledge is power.
- **Scenarios:** A guest orders a Margarita. You ask "Do you like it smooth or with a little kick?". now you can suggest Reposado vs. Blanco, salt vs. Tajín. One question, elevated experience.
- **Pitfalls:** Pushing top-shelf on every order. Upselling should feel like a recommendation, not a sales pitch.

---

## High-Volume Mindset & Strategy `[Process]`

**Multitasking, prioritization, and maintaining quality when it gets busy.**

- **Objective:** You run the bar. The bar doesn't run you. Controlling the pace is about preparation, sequencing, and composure.
- **Audit:** Managing 3 tickets and a bar guest simultaneously. Staying organized when the printer doesn't stop.
- **Reset:** When it gets hectic, reset: wipe the bar, check your rail, take a breath. Controlled resets prevent spiraling.
- **Result:** You maintain quality and composure at volume.

## Live Service Practice & Guest Scenarios `[Guest-Facing]`

- **Scenarios:** A guest says their drink is too strong. Don't argue. Ask what they'd prefer, adjust or rebuild. The goal is resolution, not debate.
- **Pitfalls:** Freezing when something goes wrong. Every problem has a fix. Stay in motion.

## Responsible Alcohol Service `[Process]`

**Local/state alcohol laws, underage policies, proper ID checking.**

- **Objective:** Know the legal line. Responsible service protects the guest, you, and the business.
- **Audit:** ID checking procedures understood. Legal drinking age verified every time, no assumptions. Expired IDs, out-of-state IDs, and fake IDs all handled per policy.
- **Reset:** When in doubt, check. When in more doubt, get the MOD.
- **Result:** You serve responsibly and confidently. No legal exposure for you or the venue.

---

## Signs of Intoxication & Preventing Overconsumption `[Process]`

**Recognize signs of intoxication and develop strategies for managing overconsumption.**

- **Objective:** Slurred speech, loss of balance, aggression, slow response. Know the signs and act early.
- **Audit:** Identify the difference between "having a good time" and "approaching the line."
- **Reset:** Strategies for slowing service: suggest food/water, pace drinks, stop serving when necessary. Stay calm, stay firm, never say "you're drunk."
- **Result:** You intervene before it becomes a problem. Proactive service beats reactive cleanup every time.

---

## Handling Difficult Situations `[Guest-Facing]`

**Refusing service, handling complaints, and de-escalating conflict professionally.**

- **The Approach:** Stay calm, stay firm, and stay solution-oriented. Refuse service without making it personal.
- **Energy:** Professional authority. You're protecting the guest, not punishing them.
- **Scenarios:** A guest pushes back after being cut off. Acknowledge their frustration, offer water or food, and hold your line. If it escalates, involve the MOD immediately.
- **Pitfalls:** Arguing with an intoxicated guest. You will not win. Disengage, involve management, move on.

## Garnish Knowledge & Ingredients `[Skill]`

**Types of garnishes, tools, syrups, purees, mixers, bitters. what's available and how each affects cocktail balance.**

- **Spec:** Wheels, wedges, twists, peels, herbs, olives, cherries, specialty. Tools: Y-peeler, citrus peeler, channel knife, paring knife.
- **Metrics:** Name and demonstrate each tool. Know syrups, purees, and bitters and how they shift a cocktail.
- **Pro Tip:** Fresh product makes better drinks. FIFO (First In, First Out), proper labeling, and refrigeration aren't busywork. They're quality control.
- **Misses:** Using a wilted garnish or expired syrup. If it's not fresh, don't serve it.

---

## Garnishing Techniques & Drink Presentation `[Skill]`

**Cut and prep all garnishes for service. Garnish 5+ cocktails with consistency.**

- **Spec:** Same garnish, same size, same placement, every time. Full drink presentation: garnish, ice level, glassware, napkin.
- **Metrics:** Speed, technique, and consistency evaluated on 5+ cocktails.
- **Pro Tip:** A well-presented drink reinforces the brand. A sloppy one undermines everything else you did right.
- **Misses:** Inconsistent garnish sizes or placement. If two Margaritas look different, the standard isn't being met.

---

## Glass Care & Polishing `[Process]`

*Location based.*

Proper handling, cleaning, polishing. Never grab by the rim. Breakage protocol: handle it safely, notify, replace. Spotless glassware is non-negotiable. If it's not clean, it doesn't leave the bar.

---

## Live Service: Speed, Efficiency & Multitasking `[Skill]`

**Work the bar during live service.**

- **Spec:** Manage the build queue and guest interaction simultaneously. Movement economy, sequencing similar builds, keeping the rail clean, no wasted steps.
- **Metrics:** Composure under volume matters more than raw speed. This is the real test before Day 5.
- **Pro Tip:** Batch similar builds. If you have three whiskey drinks, build them together. Efficiency is in the sequencing.
- **Misses:** Losing station control when it gets busy. Wipe the bar, reset the rail, stay organized. Controlled chaos, not actual chaos.

---

**Complex scenarios: splitting checks, large group tabs, comps, voids, discounts.**

## Bar Takeover & Cleanliness Standards `[Process]`

**Take over the bar from the opening bartender. Evaluate the handoff.**

- **Objective:** Assess what's stocked, what's low, what needs attention. Sanitize surfaces, clean tools, polish glassware, check garnish freshness.
- **Audit:** Deep cleaning awareness: what gets done weekly/monthly, where supplies live, who's responsible.
- **Reset:** A clean bar isn't a task. It's a standard. Maintain it throughout the entire shift.
- **Result:** The bar you take over is better than the bar you found. That's the expectation every shift.

## High-Volume Service Execution `[Skill]`

**Run the bar during live service.**

- **Spec:** Speed techniques: pour efficiency, jigger accuracy at pace, building multiple drinks simultaneously. Order prioritization: service well vs. bar guests, sequencing by build type, acknowledging the wait.
- **Metrics:** No wasted steps. Station resets between orders. Restocking during lulls. Quality doesn't drop when volume goes up.
- **Pro Tip:** Acknowledge the wait. A quick "I see you, I'll be right there" buys you 60 seconds of goodwill.
- **Misses:** Tunnel vision on tickets while ignoring bar guests. You serve the room, not just the printer.

---

## Guest Interaction & Communication `[Guest-Facing]`

**Handle all guest interaction: greetings, orders, recommendations, complaints, close-outs.**

- **The Approach:** Greeting within 30 seconds, check-back timing, professional farewell. Communicate with team: calling out tickets, coordinating with servers, flagging issues to MOD.
- **Energy:** Confident and in control. Whether it's packed or slow, the service standard doesn't change.
- **Scenarios:** A regular asks for something not on the menu. Navigate it with confidence. Offer alternatives, check with the bar, make it happen when possible.
- **Pitfalls:** Dropping communication when it's busy. The team runs on information. Keep talking.

---

## Live Scenarios & Problem Solving `[Guest-Facing]`

**Real-time pressure: rapid-fire tickets, demanding guest scenarios, curveball orders.**

- **The Approach:** Handle it. Decision-making, composure, and recovery are what matter.
- **Energy:** Calm under fire. Every problem has a fix. Stay in motion.
- **Scenarios:** A guest says their drink is wrong — resolve it live. A regular asks for something not on the menu — navigate it. Confidence over perfection.
- **Pitfalls:** Waiting for someone to step in. Today you handle it. Unless safety is at risk, the decision is yours.

---

## Closing Procedures `[Process]`

**Lead the full closing process: breakdown, cleaning, restocking, cash-out, closing checklist.**

- **Objective:** Close the bar the way you'd want to find it when you open tomorrow.
- **Audit:** Every item on the closing checklist completed. Nothing missed or done out of order.
- **Reset:** Every item on the closing checklist gets done. No "I'll get it tomorrow."
- **Result:** The bar is spotless, stocked, and ready for the next shift. That's the standard.
