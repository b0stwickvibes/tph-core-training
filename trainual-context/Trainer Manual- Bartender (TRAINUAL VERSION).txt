# 📕 TRAINER MANUAL: BARTENDER — C.O.R.E. TRAINING PROGRAM
**Three Points Hospitality**

---

This manual is your teaching reference for the Bartender C.O.R.E. 5-Day Training Program. Each day contains the tasks you are responsible for covering with your trainee on the floor.

**For shift operations (pre-shift checks, checklist printing, accountability submission), refer to the Bartender Daily Training Guide in Trainual.**

---

## 1️⃣Day 1 Training \- Orientation Phase

---

### ✴️**Tasks 1 \- 4**

## **1\. Trainee Topic: Welcome and Introduction**

**Trainee Task:** Familiarize with the training environment and personnel.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction:** Welcome the trainee warmly, emphasizing the importance of first impressions in the hospitality industry. Explain how their role as a bartender will contribute to the overall guest experience.  
* **Preparation:** Ensure the trainee has submitted any necessary paperwork and is properly clocked in before beginning the orientation.  
* **Motivation and Engagement:** Encourage the trainee to actively participate in the training process, asking questions, and sharing any relevant previous experience.  
* **Connection Building:** Introduce the trainee to key team members and discuss how building rapport with colleagues contributes to a supportive work environment.

*Note: Trainees will review this information on their own; cover the most important points (This should be brief)*

## **2\. Trainee Topic: Company Policies and Procedures**

**Trainee Task:** Review Employee Handbook starting with the company’s practice (Mission, Vision, Core), employment policies, attendance, required uniform, SCS, and training outline.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Brief Explanation:** Discuss the company’s mission, vision, and core values, explaining how these guide the actions and decisions within the establishment.  
* **Practical Application:** Provide scenarios where these policies directly influence decision-making and guest interactions.

*Note: Trainees will review this information on their own; cover the most important points (This should be brief)*

## **3\. Trainee Topic: Uniforms, Personal Hygiene, Health and Safety Regulations, Serving Alcohol**

**Trainee Task:** Understand the importance of personal appearance and hygiene, alcohol service agreements, and training.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Standards Overview:** Review the establishment’s expectations for uniforms and personal hygiene, emphasizing how these standards reflect on the brand and affect guest perceptions.  
* **Health and Safety:** Explain the health and safety regulations that must be adhered to, including safe alcohol service practices.  
* **Demonstration:** Show examples of proper uniform standards and discuss best practices for maintaining hygiene throughout the shift.  
* **Interactive Discussion:** Engage the trainee in a conversation about the importance of health and safety regulations, encouraging them to ask questions or share experiences from past roles.

## **4\. Trainee Topic: Payrolls, Tips, and Scheduling**

**Trainee Task:** Understand payroll processes, tip distribution, and scheduling.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Overview:** Discuss how paychecks, direct deposit, and tips are acquired.  
* **Demonstration:** Walk through the 7Shifts app to illustrate how to view schedules and tips; how to efficiently use 7-Shifts, and request time off.  
* **Feedback:** Discuss common challenges servers face regarding scheduling, how to pick up, drop, and swap shifts.

**Branch Portal:**

### ✴️**Tasks 5 \- 7**

## **5\. Trainee Topic: Spirit Categories Overview**

**Trainee Task:** Introduce major spirit categories and discuss key characteristics of each.

#### **Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction to Spirit Categories:** Begin by introducing the trainee to the six major spirit categories: Vodka, Gin, Rum, Tequila, Whiskey, and Liqueurs. Provide a brief history and background of each category to give context to their significance in bartending.  
* **Key Characteristics:** Discuss the defining characteristics of each spirit, such as flavor profiles, base ingredients, and typical alcohol content. For example, explain how vodka is often neutral and versatile, while whiskey is rich and complex with notes that vary depending on aging and grain used.  
* **Brand Familiarization:** Introduce the trainee to popular brands within each spirit category that are available at your establishment. Highlight any house favorites or unique offerings that might distinguish your bar from others.  
* **Practical Tasting and Smelling:** If possible, guide the trainee through a practical session where they can taste (or smell) and compare different spirits to better understand their distinct characteristics. Encourage them to take notes on flavor profiles, aroma, and texture to reinforce their learning.  
* **Engagement and Q\&A:** Conclude by encouraging the trainee to ask questions or share their thoughts on the different spirits. Use this as an opportunity to clarify any uncertainties and ensure they have a solid understanding of the major spirit categories.

## **6\. Trainee Topic: Product Knowledge**

**Trainee Task:** Review available products at the establishment, including flavor profiles and popular brands.

#### **Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction:** Start with an overview of the products available at the bar, focusing on popular brands, house specialties, and unique offerings.  
* **Flavor Profiles:** Explain the flavor profiles of key products, emphasizing how understanding these profiles helps in recommending drinks to guests.  
* **Interactive Learning:** Encourage the trainee to sample non-alcoholic versions of mixers and syrups to familiarize themselves with the flavors.  
* **Practical Application:** Discuss how product knowledge directly impacts the guest experience and upselling opportunities.

## **7\. Trainee Topic: The Grand Tour**

**Trainee Task:** Familiarize with the layout of the Front of House (FOH) and Back of House (BOH) areas.

#### **Trainer Instructions:**

* **Comprehensive Tour:** Guide the trainee through both the Front of House and Back of House areas. In the FOH, show them key locations such as the bar(s), wells, POS stations, beer coolers, host stand, bathroom locations, entryways, and emergency exits. Continue into the BOH, highlighting essential areas including the drink station, walk-in coolers/freezers, lockers, ice machines, employee bathrooms, manager's office, kitchen/dish pit, storage rooms, and back entryways.  
* **Explanation and Functionality:** As you tour each area, explain the importance of knowing these locations for efficient service and guest interaction in the FOH. Similarly, discuss the purpose of each BOH area and how it contributes to the smooth operations behind the bar.  
* **Memory Reinforcement:** Periodically ask the trainee to recall and point out specific locations in both the FOH and BOH, reinforcing their familiarity with the layout. This will help solidify their understanding and ensure they can navigate the space efficiently during their shifts.

### ✴️**Tasks 8 \- 10**

## **8\. Trainee Topic: Introduction to Bartending**

**Trainee Task:** Gain a basic understanding of bartending history, duties, and key policies.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Brief Overview:** Provide a brief history of bartending, touching on its evolution and importance in hospitality.  
* **Duties and Responsibilities:** Discuss the core duties of a bartender, including drink preparation, customer service, and maintaining cleanliness.  
* **Key Policies:** Review important policies specific to bartending, such as responsible alcohol service and guest interaction standards.  
* **Engagement:** Encourage the trainee to share their understanding of bartending and how it aligns with their role.

## **9\. Trainee Topic: SCS & The Basics**

**Trainee Task:** Understand the importance of 'Service, Consistency, Speed' in bartending.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Concept Introduction:** Explain the critical role of Service, Consistency, and Speed (SCS) in delivering an exceptional guest experience.  
* **Practical Examples:** Provide examples of how each element of SCS influences daily operations, such as how consistency in drink preparation affects guest satisfaction.  
* **Demonstration:** Show how you maintain SCS during a busy shift, emphasizing multitasking and efficiency.  
* **Engagement:** Have the trainee discuss how they can implement SCS in their daily work, reinforcing the importance of these principles.

## **10\. Trainee Topic: Building Cocktails and Basic Mixing Methods**

**Trainee Task:** Learn the importance of balance and dilution in cocktails, and basic mixing methods like shaking and stirring.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Balance and Dilution:** Explain the importance of balance in cocktails and how proper dilution (through the role of ice) impacts the drink's flavor and texture.  
* **Mixing Methods:** Demonstrate basic mixing methods, including shaking and stirring, and explain when each method is appropriate.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice these methods, providing guidance on technique and corrections where necessary.

**Feedback:** Offer constructive feedback on the trainee's technique, emphasizing the importance of precision in cocktail preparation.

### ✴️**Tasks 11 \- 14**

## **11\. Trainee Topic: Essential Bar Tools**

**Trainee Task:** Learn about each bar tool, including proper usage and care.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Tool Introduction:** Introduce the trainee to essential bar tools, such as shakers, strainers, jiggers, bar spoons, and muddlers. Discuss the function of each tool.  
* **Demonstration:** Demonstrate the correct usage and care of each tool, explaining common mistakes and how to avoid them.  
* **Interactive Learning:** Encourage the trainee to handle the tools, practicing basic techniques under your supervision.  
* **Maintenance Tips:** Share tips on how to properly maintain and store bar tools to ensure their longevity and effectiveness.

## **12\. Trainee Topic: Glassware Types**

**Trainee Task:** Examine various types of glassware, their appropriate uses, and designated storage locations.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Glassware Overview:** Review the different types of glassware used in the bar, such as rocks glasses, martini glasses, and highballs.  
* **Usage Explanation:** Explain the appropriate uses for each type of glassware and how they enhance the guest experience.  
* **Storage Locations:** Show where each type of glassware is stored, emphasizing the importance of proper organization and accessibility.  
* **Practical Application:** Have the trainee identify and select the correct glassware for specific drinks, reinforcing their understanding.  
  * **Cantina Trainers Reference:** [Cantina Glassware](https://app.trainual.com/f9e05a9e-ccec-463e-beae-d1c5489f4c52/courses/4375036)

## **13\. Trainee Topic: Introduction to Bar Layout**

**Trainee Task:** Review the functions of the soda gun, 3-tier sinks, hand sinks, speed rails, and back bottle display.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Bar Layout Overview:** Walk the trainee through the bar layout, focusing on key areas like the soda gun, 3-tier sinks, hand sinks, speed rails, and back bottle display.  
* **Function Explanation:** Explain how each area contributes to the overall workflow and efficiency behind the bar.  
* **Interactive Discussion:** Encourage the trainee to ask questions and discuss how they would use each area during their shift.  
* **Memory Reinforcement:** Periodically quiz the trainee on the functions of each area to reinforce their understanding.

## **14\. Trainee Topic: Efficient Bar Setup**

**Trainee Task:** Observe and review the proper bar setup, including cubbies, locations of all dry disposable goods and cleaning supplies, backup bottles, and overall well organization.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Demonstration:** Show the trainee how to set up the bar efficiently, ensuring all necessary tools, supplies, and backup items are organized and accessible.  
* **Efficiency Tips:** Provide tips on maintaining an organized workspace, such as grouping similar items together and keeping frequently used items within reach.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice setting up the bar under your guidance, offering corrections and suggestions for improvement.

**Feedback:** Offer constructive feedback on their setup, emphasizing the importance of efficiency and organization in maintaining smooth operations.

### ✴️**Tasks 15 \- 16**

## **15\. Trainee Topic: Shadow & Observe**

**Trainee Task:** Spend time behind the bar closely watching your trainer, paying attention to their techniques, customer interactions, and workflow.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Observation Period:** Allow the trainee to shadow you for at least one hour, observing your techniques, customer interactions, and overall workflow.  
* **Critical Analysis:** As you work, point out key aspects of your actions, such as multitasking, customer engagement, and maintaining cleanliness.  
* **Interactive Discussion:** After the observation period, engage the trainee in a discussion about what they noticed, addressing any questions or concerns.  
* **Feedback:** Provide feedback on the trainee’s observations, reinforcing the importance of the techniques they observed.

## **16\. Trainee Topic: Classic Cocktail & Shot Familiarization**

**Trainee Task:** Review the importance of memorizing all shots and classic cocktail recipes. If applicable, review specialty cocktails on your venue’s menu.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Classic Cocktails:** Emphasize the importance of memorizing classic cocktail recipes, such as the Old Fashioned, Martini, and Margarita. Explain how these form the foundation of more complex drinks.  
* **Specialty Cocktails:** If your venue has specialty cocktails, introduce these to the trainee and discuss the unique ingredients or techniques used in their preparation.  
* **Memory Techniques:** Offer strategies for memorizing recipes, such as grouping similar drinks together or using mnemonic devices.

**Practice Session:** Encourage the trainee to practice making these cocktails, providing guidance and feedback as they work.

## 2️⃣Day 2 Training – Opening/Mid-Shift

---

### ✴️**Tasks 1 \- 4**

## **1\. Trainee Topic: Bar Opening Procedures**

**Trainee Task:** Walk through the bar opening checklist and practice setting up the bar for service.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction to Opening Procedures:** Begin by explaining the significance of following a consistent opening checklist to ensure the bar is properly prepared for service. Emphasize how a thorough setup contributes to efficiency and smooth operations throughout the shift.  
* **Checklist Walkthrough:** Guide the trainee through the bar opening checklist step-by-step, pointing out key tasks such as restocking supplies, setting up the stations, and ensuring cleanliness.  
* **Hands-On Setup:** Allow the trainee to practice setting up the bar for service under your supervision. Observe their attention to detail and provide guidance to ensure they understand the importance of each task.  
* **Review and Feedback:** After the setup is complete, review the trainee’s work and provide constructive feedback, highlighting areas where they performed well and areas where improvement is needed.

## **2\. Trainee Topic: POS System Orientation**

**Trainee Task:** Be introduced to the POS system and understand its importance in bar operations.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction to the POS System:** Begin by explaining the role of the POS system in bar operations, including how it is used for order entry, payment processing, and inventory management.  
* **System Overview:** Provide a detailed overview of the POS system (Explain what Toast is), demonstrating how to navigate the interface, input orders, opening cash register, splitting checks, finding checks, and access different functions. Highlight the importance of accuracy and efficiency when using the system.  
* **Q\&A Session:** Encourage the trainee to ask questions about the POS system, clarifying any uncertainties they may have.

## **3\. Trainee Topic: POS Hands-on Practice**

**Trainee Task:** Practice using the POS system, inputting orders and modifications.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Hands-On Demonstration:** Demonstrate how to input various types of orders and modifications into the POS system, including special requests and dietary restrictions.  
* **Practice Session:** Allow the trainee to practice entering orders into the POS system, guiding them through different scenarios, such as splitting bills or applying discounts.  
* **Real-Time Feedback:** Provide immediate feedback on their inputs, ensuring they understand how to use the system accurately and efficiently.  
* **Troubleshooting Tips:** Offer basic troubleshooting tips, such as what to do if an order is entered incorrectly or if the system experiences a glitch.

## **4\. Trainee Topic: POS Troubleshooting**

**Trainee Task:** Learn about common POS issues and basic troubleshooting techniques.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Common Issues Overview:** Discuss the most common issues that can arise with the POS system, such as freezing, incorrect order entry, or payment processing errors.  
* **Troubleshooting Techniques:** Teach the trainee basic troubleshooting techniques:  
  * Changing & Replacing Receipt Paper  
  * Opening Cash Register  
  * Printing to another POS  
  * Explain the cash register will not operate when paper is out  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to simulate troubleshooting scenarios, guiding them through the steps to resolve each issue.  
* **Feedback and Support:** Provide feedback on their problem-solving approach and offer additional tips to improve their troubleshooting skills.

### ✴️**Tasks 5 \- 8**

## **5\. Trainee Topic: Jigger Types and Proper Pouring Techniques**

**Trainee Task:** Learn about different jigger sizes and styles, and practice accurate jigger pouring techniques.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction to Jiggers:** Introduce the trainee to various jigger sizes and styles, explaining how each is used for measuring different types of spirits and ingredients.  
* **Pouring Techniques Demonstration:** Demonstrate the proper technique for using a jigger to ensure accurate measurements. Discuss the importance of precision in pouring to maintain consistency in cocktail preparation.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice pouring with different jiggers, focusing on achieving consistent measurements. Provide guidance on correcting any errors in technique.  
* **Reinforcement:** Emphasize the importance of using jiggers correctly to avoid over-pouring or under-pouring, which can affect the quality and cost-efficiency of the drinks.

## **6\. Trainee Topic: Measuring Techniques**

**Trainee Task:** Emphasize accurate measurements and consistency, and understand the impact of under-pouring vs. over-pouring.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Importance of Accuracy:** Discuss the significance of accurate measurements in maintaining the quality and balance of cocktails. Explain how consistency in measurements ensures that every guest receives the same high-quality experience.  
* **Impact of Pouring Errors:** Explain the consequences of under-pouring (e.g., weak drinks, customer dissatisfaction) and over-pouring (e.g., waste, increased costs). Use examples to illustrate the financial and repetitional impact of inconsistent pouring.  
* **Practice Session:** Allow the trainee to practice measuring various ingredients, focusing on consistency and accuracy. Observe their technique and provide feedback to help them improve.  
* **Reinforcement:** Reinforce the need for precision in every pour, highlighting how it contributes to both customer satisfaction and efficient bar operations.

## **7\. Trainee Topic: Building a Cocktail**

**Trainee Task:** Understand the elements and balance of flavors for basic cocktail structure.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction to Cocktail Structure:** Discuss the fundamental elements of a well-balanced cocktail, including the base spirit, modifiers, sweeteners, and bitters. Explain how each element contributes to the overall flavor profile.  
* **Flavor Balance:** Teach the trainee how to balance flavors in a cocktail, focusing on achieving the right mix of sweetness, acidity, and bitterness.  
* **Hands-On Demonstration:** Demonstrate the process of building a simple cocktail, explaining each step and why certain ingredients are added in a specific order.  
* **Practice Session:** Allow the trainee to build their own cocktail under your supervision, guiding them in balancing the flavors correctly.  
* **Feedback and Tasting:** After the trainee has completed their cocktail, taste it together and provide feedback on the balance of flavors and overall execution.

## **8\. Trainee Topic: Hands-on Tool Familiarization**

**Trainee Task:** Engage in a practical session focused on using bar tools and understanding their primary functions.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Tool Overview:** Introduce the trainee to the essential bar tools, such as shakers, strainers, muddlers, and jiggers. Explain the primary function of each tool and how it is used in cocktail preparation.  
* **Hands-On Demonstration:** Demonstrate the proper use of each tool, highlighting key techniques and best practices for achieving the desired results.  
* **Practice Session:** Allow the trainee to practice using each tool, guiding them through various tasks such as shaking, stirring, muddling, and straining. Observe their technique and provide feedback.  
* **Tool Maintenance:** Discuss the importance of proper tool maintenance and cleaning to ensure longevity and hygiene in the bar setting.

### ✴️**Tasks 9 & 10**

## **9\. Trainee Topic: Introduction to Shaking Techniques**

**Trainee Task:** Learn the basics of shaking cocktails and understand when to use this method. Participate in a quick demonstration followed by hands-on practice in building cocktails.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Shaking Basics:** Begin by explaining the purpose of shaking a cocktail, such as blending ingredients, chilling the drink, and creating the desired texture. Discuss when shaking is preferred over other methods like stirring.  
* **Demonstration:** Demonstrate the correct technique for shaking a cocktail, focusing on the grip, shaking motion, and timing.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice shaking cocktails under your supervision, guiding them in achieving the correct technique and consistency.  
* **Feedback and Tasting:** Provide feedback on their shaking technique and taste the final product together, discussing how the shaking process affected the drink’s texture and flavor.

## **10\. Trainee Topic: Introduction to Stirring Techniques**

**Trainee Task:** Learn the basics of stirring cocktails and understand when to use this method. Participate in a quick demonstration and hands-on practice in building cocktails.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Stirring Basics:** Explain the purpose of stirring a cocktail, such as maintaining clarity and minimizing dilution while properly mixing the ingredients. Discuss when stirring is the preferred method over shaking.  
* **Demonstration:** Demonstrate the correct technique for stirring a cocktail, focusing on the grip, stirring motion, and the appropriate length of time.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice stirring cocktails under your supervision, ensuring they use the correct technique to achieve the desired results.  
* **Feedback and Tasting:** Provide feedback on their stirring technique and taste the final product together, discussing how the stirring process affected the drink’s clarity and flavor.

### ✴️**Tasks 11 \- 14**

## **11\. Trainee Topic: Pouring Orientation**

**Trainee Task:** Introduction to the art of free pouring: Learn when it's acceptable to free pour, practice pour counting, and understand the use of a jigger.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Free Pouring Overview:** Explain the concept of free pouring, when it is acceptable to use this technique, and the importance of maintaining consistency through pour counting.  
* **Demonstration:** Demonstrate the free pouring technique, focusing on pour counting to ensure accurate measurements. Highlight the situations where using a jigger is preferred for precision.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice free pouring and pour counting under your supervision. Provide tips on achieving accuracy and consistency in their pours.  
* **Reinforcement:** Emphasize the importance of accuracy in free pouring and the role of the jigger in maintaining consistent drink quality.

## **12\. Trainee Topic: Bottle Holding Techniques**

**Trainee Task:** Master bottle holding techniques, including learning how to hold single bottles, manage multiple bottles with one hand, and execute these methods confidently and efficiently during free pouring and/or when using jiggers.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction to Bottle Holding:** Begin by explaining the importance of proper bottle holding techniques for both efficiency and safety behind the bar.  
* **Demonstration:** Demonstrate how to hold and pour from single bottles, as well as how to manage multiple bottles with one hand. Show how these techniques apply during both free pouring and when using jiggers.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice holding and pouring from bottles, both singly and in multiples. Observe their technique and provide feedback to ensure they are executing the methods confidently and efficiently.  
* **Reinforcement:** Reinforce the need for proper bottle handling to maintain speed, accuracy, and professionalism behind the bar.

## **13\. Trainee Topic: Free-Pour Practice**

**Trainee Task:** Practice free pouring using provided test bottles and tester measuring tools. Learn how to use a jigger appropriately and practice calibrating techniques used behind the bar.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Introduction to Free Pour Practice:** Explain the importance of practicing free pouring to develop accuracy and consistency in drink preparation. Discuss how using tester measuring tools can help calibrate their pouring technique.  
* **Demonstration:** Demonstrate how to practice free pouring with test bottles and measuring tools. Show the trainee how to calibrate their pours and ensure they are consistently hitting the correct measurements.  
* **Hands-On Practice:** Allow the trainee to practice free pouring and using a jigger under your supervision. Guide them through the calibration process to refine their technique.  
* **Feedback and Improvement:** Provide feedback on their pouring accuracy and offer tips on how to improve their consistency. Encourage regular practice to develop muscle memory for accurate pouring.

## **14\. Trainee Topic: Shadow & Observe**

**Trainee Task:** Spend time behind the bar closely watching your trainer, paying attention to their techniques, customer interactions, and workflow to gain practical insights and understand the standards that are expected.

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Observation Setup:** Arrange for the trainee to shadow you during a live shift, allowing them to observe your techniques, customer interactions, and workflow in real time.  
* **Guided Observation:** While working, explain your actions and decisions to the trainee, providing insights into why certain techniques are used and how to handle various situations behind the bar.  
* **Q\&A:** Encourage the trainee to ask questions during the observation period, helping them understand the rationale behind each action.  
* **Reflection:** After the observation period, engage the trainee in a discussion about what they observed, what they learned, and how they can apply these insights to their own work.

## **6\. Trainee Topic: High Volume Bartending Strategies**

**Trainee Task:** Discussion on efficiency techniques for busy periods: Practice multitasking, prioritization, and customer acknowledgement

**Trainer Instructions:**

* **Efficiency Techniques:**
