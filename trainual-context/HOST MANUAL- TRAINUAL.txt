## 📚Position Overview

---

### 🔆**Role Overview**

As a host, you are the first point of contact for our guests. Your role is crucial in creating a positive first impression. You will greet arriving guests with a warm smile and friendly attitude, welcome them into the restaurant, and seat them at appropriate tables. Your interaction sets the tone for the guests' experience, so it is essential to be pleasant, welcoming, and professional.

### 🫵**Personal Appearance**

### **Dress Code:**

Your overall image reflects our restaurant's image. The impression you create can enhance or detract from our establishment's reputation. As you handle our guests' needs, it is essential to maintain an appearance of cleanliness and professionalism at all times. Remember the following:

1. **Clothing**  
   * Keep your clothing neat and clean at all times. Arriving outside of the dress code is unacceptable.  
   * Do not wear unpressed or dirty clothing, and ensure your hair is well-groomed.  
   * A smile is an essential part of your attire.

2. **Scent and Hygiene**  
   * Avoid wearing scented lotions or perfumes, as they can cling to glassware and detract from the food experience.  
   * Chewing gum or eating in public areas is prohibited.

3. **Professional Attitude**  
   * When you walk through the door, remember: "YOU ARE ON." Your attitude should be contagious and enthusiastic.  
   * Enter and leave the building in full dress attire.

## 📝Uniform Guidelines

### 👗**Uniform Requirements**

* Closed-toe shoes with grip

### ✍️**Enter Guest Information**

When entering guest information, it's essential to ensure accuracy and thoroughness. If the guest's details are not already in the system, you'll need to manually input their information to provide the best possible service and maintain our guest database.

* **First & Last Name:** Record the guest's full name to personalize their experience and facilitate future visits.  
* **Telephone Number:** Input the guest’s phone number to enable communication for reservations, confirmations, and any necessary follow-ups.  
* **E-mail Address:** Collect the guest’s e-mail to send reservation confirmations, special offers, and updates about upcoming events.  
* **Special Notes:** Make a note of any specific requests or requirements the guest may have, such as a birthday celebration, the need for a highchair or wheelchair accessibility, preference for a booth, or a desire for outdoor seating. This ensures that their experience is tailored to their needs and expectations

### 📅**Confirming, Editing, & Canceling Reservations**

Managing reservations accurately is crucial for providing smooth and efficient service. Whether you need to confirm, edit, or cancel a reservation, follow these steps to ensure the guest’s request is handled promptly and correctly.

1. **Find the Reservation in the Resy App:** Start by locating the specific reservation in the Resy app using the guest's name or reservation details.  
2. **Click the Reservation Name:** Select the reservation by clicking on the guest's name to access the reservation details.  
3. **Change the Status Based on Guest Instructions:** Adjust the reservation status according to the guest's request—whether they are confirming, canceling, or updating their reservation. Options include marking the reservation as canceled, confirmed, or noting if a message was left.  
4. **Click Out of the Reservation:** Once the changes are made, click out of the reservation to save the updates and return to the main reservations screen.

## 🍽️Seating Guests

---

### 👋**Greet & Inquire**

When seating guests, it’s important to create a welcoming first impression that sets the tone for their entire dining experience. Greet arriving guests warmly and with a genuine smile. Politely inquire if they have a reservation or if they need assistance finding a table. If they have any questions or need further information, be ready to assist them promptly and courteously. Guide them to their table with confidence, ensuring they feel comfortable and well taken care of from the moment they enter the establishment. This initial interaction is key to making guests feel valued and enhancing their overall experience.

### 📄**Check Reservations**

For guests with reservations, begin by consulting the reservation sheet to confirm their booking and assigned table. Ensure that the table is properly set and ready for their arrival. Once everything is in order, escort the guests to their designated table with a warm and professional demeanor. Make sure they are comfortably seated, and take a moment to ensure they have everything they need. This attention to detail reinforces the guests’ expectations and enhances their overall dining experience.

### 🗣️**Engage and Pace**

Maintaining a steady pace and engaging in friendly conversation with guests is essential to creating a welcoming and comfortable atmosphere. As you interact with guests, ensure that your pace matches theirs, allowing them to feel relaxed and unrushed. Engage in light, friendly conversation to make them feel valued and at ease. This approach helps build rapport and enhances their overall experience, making them feel truly welcomed and cared for throughout their visit.

### 🙌**Polite Farewell**

After seating guests, it’s important to leave them with a positive impression by offering a polite and warm farewell. Once they are comfortably seated, wish them an enjoyable meal with a phrase like, “Enjoy your meal.” This simple, courteous gesture reinforces your hospitality and leaves guests feeling appreciated and well cared for as they begin their dining experience.​

### 📢**Notify Staff**

After seating guests, it’s crucial to ensure a smooth and coordinated service by promptly informing the relevant team members. Notify the assigned server, service assistant (busser), and kitchen staff of the newly seated guests. This communication ensures that everyone is aware and prepared to provide timely and attentive service, contributing to a seamless dining experience for the guests.

### 🌟**Demonstration, Pointers, & Tips**

# **Demonstration, Pointers & Tips:**

### **1\. Greet Guests Warmly:**

* **First Impressions Matter:** A genuine greeting sets the tone for the entire dining experience. Smile, make eye contact, and offer a sincere welcome.  
* **Acknowledge Immediately:** Even when busy, acknowledge arriving guests with "I'll be right with you" to ensure they feel noticed and valued.

### **2\. Assess the Situation:**

* **Reservation Check:** Confirm reservation details including name and party size. For walk-ins, quickly assess availability and provide accurate wait times.  
* **Special Requests:** Inquire about seating preferences (booth, table, patio, quiet area) and accommodate when possible to enhance guest comfort.

### **3\. Manage the Seating Flow:**

* **Balancing Sections:** Distribute guests evenly across server sections to ensure consistent service quality throughout the restaurant.  
* **Timing is Key:** Pace the seating to prevent overwhelming servers and kitchen staff, maintaining service quality during busy periods.

### 

### **4\. Escort Guests with Confidence:**

* **Lead the Way:** Walk at a comfortable pace, clearly directing guests with phrases like "Right this way" or "Follow me, please."  
* **Engage in Light Conversation:** Create a welcoming atmosphere with brief, friendly conversation about their day or occasion while escorting them to their table.

### 

### **5\. Seat Guests Gracefully:**

* **Assist When Appropriate:** Offer chair assistance for elderly guests, children, or those with mobility challenges.  
* **Present Menus Professionally:** Place menus with the logo facing guests and inform them their server will arrive shortly.

### 

### **6\. Inform the Guests:**

* **Introduce the Server:** Share the server's name when possible, creating a personal connection and setting service expectations.  
* **Highlight Specials:** Briefly mention daily specials and any potential wait times if the kitchen is experiencing high volume.

### 

### **7\. Offer Additional Assistance:**

* **Address Immediate Needs:** Proactively ask about requirements for high chairs, booster seats, or other accommodations.  
* **Ensure Table Readiness:** Verify the table is clean, stable, and properly set before seating guests.

### 

### **8\. Maintain a Positive Attitude:**

* **Stay Calm Under Pressure:** Project confidence and composure during busy periods to maintain a pleasant atmosphere for guests and staff.  
* **Handle Issues Proactively:** Address seating problems with a sincere apology, offer alternatives, and provide clear timelines for resolution.

### 

### **9\. Maintain Continuous Awareness:**

* **Monitor Table Status:** Track tables approaching the end of their meal to prepare for efficient turnover and minimize wait times.  
* **Remain Visible:** Stay near the host stand or entrance to promptly assist arriving guests and manage the waiting area.

### 

### **10\. Bid Farewell:**

* **Express Gratitude:** Thank departing guests with "Thank you for dining with us; we hope to see you again soon" to create a lasting positive impression.  
* **Seek Feedback:** When appropriate, inquire about their experience to demonstrate care for their satisfaction and gather valuable insights for improvement.

## ⏳Managing Wait Times & Waitlist

---

### ⏱️**Estimating Wait Times**

Effectively managing wait times and the waitlist is key to maintaining a positive guest experience, even during busy periods. Follow these guidelines to ensure guests are informed and comfortable while they wait.

#### **Examples of estimated wait times & quotes you can use:**

* **Tables Occupied for 30-60 Minutes:** Quote a 15-20 minute wait time.  
  * Example: "It looks like your wait will be about 15-20 minutes. We appreciate your patience\!"  
* **Tables Occupied for 60-90 Minutes:** Quote a 30-minute wait time.  
  * Example: "The current wait is around 30 minutes. You're welcome to relax at the bar while you wait."  
* **Tables Occupied for 90-120 Minutes:** Quote a 45-minute wait time.  
  * Example: "We’re estimating a 45-minute wait. Would you like to grab a drink at the bar in the meantime?"  
* **Fully Booked with Few Upcoming Openings:** Quote a 60-minute wait time.  
  * Example: "We’re quite busy tonight, so the wait is about 60 minutes. We’ll keep you updated if anything opens up sooner."  
* **Large Parties Waiting:** Quote a 45-60 minute wait time.  
  * Example: "For a party of your size, we’re estimating a 45-60 minute wait. We’ll do our best to accommodate you as quickly as possible."  
* **Quick Turnover Anticipated:** Quote a 10-minute wait time.  
  * Example: "We have some tables finishing up shortly; the wait should be about 10 minutes."

### 📝**Waitlist Management**

When managing the waitlist, begin by collecting the guest’s full name and cell phone number. This information is essential for efficiently managing the queue and ensuring you can promptly notify guests when their table is ready. Being organized and clear in your waitlist management helps maintain a positive guest experience, even during busy times.

### 🍹**Guest Accommodation**

While guests are waiting for their table, enhance their experience by suggesting the bar area as a comfortable option to enjoy a drink. Politely inform them that they are welcome to relax at the bar while their table is being prepared. This thoughtful suggestion not only keeps guests engaged but also adds to their overall enjoyment during the wait.

### 💬**Communication**

Effective communication is key to managing guest expectations and ensuring a smooth experience. Always provide clear and accurate information about wait times and available options to guests. This transparency helps guests plan their time and feel more comfortable during their wait.

Throughout the process, remember to maintain a professional and courteous demeanor, as your tone and approach significantly impact the guest’s perception of their overall experience. Clear, respectful communication fosters trust and enhances the guest’s overall satisfaction.​⬤

## 👉Common Rebuttals

---

### 📃**Common Objections & Suggested Rebuttals**

#### **As a host at Three Points Hospitality, you will encounter various guest objections or concerns. Handling these situations with confidence and professionalism is essential to ensure a positive guest experience. Below are 15 common objections and suggested rebuttals to help you address them effectively.**

#### 

**"I didn't know there was a dress code."**

* **Rebuttal:** Politely inform the guest, "We aim to maintain a certain atmosphere in our restaurant, which is why we have a dress code. However, we appreciate your understanding and are happy to accommodate you as best as we can today."

**"Why is there a wait when there are empty tables?"**

* **Rebuttal:** Explain to the guest, "We stagger seating to ensure that our kitchen and service staff can provide the best possible dining experience for each guest. We appreciate your patience and will seat you as soon as possible."

**"I don't see why I need a reservation."**

* **Rebuttal:** Respond with, "Reservations help us manage the flow of guests and ensure that everyone receives timely service. While we do accept walk-ins, having a reservation guarantees your table at your preferred time."

**"I wasn't informed about the cancellation policy."**

* **Rebuttal:** Kindly say, "I apologize if there was any confusion. Our cancellation policy is designed to ensure that we can accommodate as many guests as possible. If you have any concerns, I'm happy to discuss them further or involve a manager."

**"I didn't receive the service I expected."**

* **Rebuttal:** Acknowledge the guest's concern by saying, "I'm sorry to hear that your experience didn't meet your expectations. Please let me know how we can make it right or if you'd like to speak with a manager."

**"The menu is too expensive."**

* **Rebuttal:** Address the concern by explaining, "We strive to provide high-quality dishes made with the finest ingredients, which can affect pricing. We believe our menu offers excellent value for the quality and experience we provide."

**"I have dietary restrictions; what can I eat?"**

* **Rebuttal:** Reassure the guest by saying, "We are happy to accommodate dietary restrictions. Please let me know your specific needs, and I can recommend suitable options or consult with our chef for personalized suggestions."

**"I can't find parking."**

* **Rebuttal:** Offer assistance by saying, "I understand parking can be challenging. We recommend using the nearby parking garage or street parking. If you need further assistance, I'm happy to provide directions or contact our valet team."

**"Why is the wait time so long?"**

* **Rebuttal:** Calmly explain, "Our goal is to provide each guest with an exceptional dining experience, which sometimes requires a little extra time. We appreciate your patience and are doing our best to accommodate everyone as quickly as possible."

**"We have a large party; can we be seated together?"**

* **Rebuttal:** Inform the guest, "We strive to accommodate large parties whenever possible. Let me check our current seating arrangements and see how we can best accommodate your group. It may take a little longer to arrange, but we will do our best to seat you together."

**"Can you accommodate additional guests in our party?"**

* **Rebuttal:** Respond with, "We would love to accommodate your additional guests. Let me see what we can do with our current seating arrangements. Please bear with us as we work to make the necessary adjustments to ensure everyone is comfortable."

**"I don't like the table location."**

* **Rebuttal:** Offer a solution by saying, "I understand. Let me check if we have any other tables available that might be more to your liking. It might take a few minutes, but I will do my best to accommodate your preference."

**"I wasn't greeted promptly."**

* **Rebuttal:** Apologize sincerely, "I apologize for the wait. We strive to greet all our guests promptly, and I'm sorry we didn't meet that expectation. How can I assist you now?"

**"The restaurant is too noisy."**

* **Rebuttal:** Acknowledge the concern, "I understand that the noise level can be a bit high during busy times. If you'd like, I can see if there is a quieter area available for you to enjoy your meal."

**"I have been waiting too long for my order."**

* **Rebuttal:** Address the issue by saying, "I apologize for the delay. Let me check with the kitchen to see what's causing the holdup, and I'll get back to you with an update as soon as possible."

| These rebuttals are crafted to address common guest concerns while maintaining the high standards of service at Three Points Hospitality. Adjust them as necessary to fit specific situations and guest needs, always prioritizing a courteous and helpful demeanor. |

## 🧼Maintain Cleanliness

---

Pre-bussing is key to maintaining a clean and efficient dining environment, enhancing both the guest experience and service flow. Regularly clear away unnecessary items to keep tables tidy.
