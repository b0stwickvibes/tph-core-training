## 👗Personal Appearance

---

### 📖**Dress Code Guidelines**

Your overall image reflects our restaurant's image. The impression you create can enhance or detract from our establishment's reputation. Maintain an appearance of cleanliness and professionalism at all times.

1. **Clothing:**  
   * Keep your clothing neat and clean at all times. Arriving outside of the dress code is unacceptable.  
   * Do not wear unpressed or dirty clothing, and ensure your hair is well-groomed.  
   * A smile is an essential part of your attire.  
2. **Scent and Hygiene:**  
   * Avoid wearing scented lotions or perfumes, as they can cling to glassware and detract from the food experience.  
   * Chewing gum or eating in public areas is prohibited.  
3. **Professional Attitude:**  
   * When you walk through the door, remember: "YOU ARE ON." Your attitude should be contagious and enthusiastic.  
   * Enter and leave the building in full dress attire.  
4. **Required Items:**  
   * Always carry at least three pens (blue or black), a wine key, and a bank of $30 in change, e.g., $15 in $1s and $15 in $5s.

### 👕**Dining Room Dress Code**

**Shirts:** Three Points Hospitality shirt or polo.

**Shoes:** Black non-slip shoes, no patterns.

**Pants:** Dark jeans without holes or designs, paired with a matching belt.

**Appearance:** Maintain clean and well-groomed hair. Long hair must be tied back. Ensure hands and fingernails are well-groomed, and facial hair is neat and trimmed.

**Makeup:** Females are expected to wear makeup that is comfortable for them and the guests.

**Accessories:** Limit cologne, perfume, and jewelry. No earrings longer than 1 inch, and no unauthorized hats or buttons. Avoid visible tongue rings or excessive piercings during your shift.

**Phones:** Cell phones must not be used on the restaurant floor at any time.

## 💡Suggestive Selling Approach

### 🌠**Enhance the Guest Experience**

As a server, your ability to make personalized recommendations can greatly enhance the guest experience, turning a simple meal into a memorable dining occasion. By tailoring suggestions to individual preferences, offering thoughtful pairings, and encouraging customization, you can create a unique and engaging experience for each guest. Use descriptive language to bring the menu to life, helping guests envision the flavors and textures of their choices.

* Offer pairings to complement meals.

**Example:** "Since you enjoyed our Cabernet Sauvignon, may I suggest our perfectly paired pepper-crusted filet mignon? The wine's tannins beautifully complement the steak's rich flavor."

As a server, one of your key roles is to drive sales while enhancing the guest experience. By strategically highlighting specials and premium items, suggesting upgrades like top-shelf liquors, and bundling items into attractive package deals, you can boost sales effectively. Additionally, cross-selling between courses provides guests with a fuller experience while increasing their overall satisfaction. For instance, “Would you like to start with our chef’s special appetizer trio? It’s a great way to sample our most popular starters at a value price.” These techniques not only contribute to increased sales but also enrich the dining experience for your guests.

* Highlight specials and premium items.  
* Suggest upgrades (e.g., top-shelf liquors).  
* Bundle items for attractive package deals.  
* Cross-sell between courses.

**Example:** "Would you like to start with our chef's special appetizer trio? It's a great way to sample our most popular starters at a value price."

### 🤗**Building Rapport**

Building rapport with guests is crucial for a memorable dining experience and repeat business. As a server, connecting through enthusiasm, humor, and active listening can enhance their visit. Show excitement about menu items, engage in light-hearted conversation, and offer samples to create a welcoming atmosphere. Remember, guests want to spend money, enjoy their meal, and rely on you to meet their needs. For example, offer a small sample of a dessert like our delicious chocolate lava cake. This approach builds rapport and enhances the guest experience.

* Show genuine enthusiasm about menu items.  
* Use humor to create a pleasant atmosphere.  
* Practice active listening to understand guest needs.  
* Offer samples to engage guests.

*Remember, not every guest will buy a bottle of wine, appetizer, or dessert. However, we know three facts about every guest:*

* They are planning to spend money.

### 💯**Best Practices for Suggestive Selling**

Mastering timing, product knowledge, personalization, and the use of visual aids are key to elevating the guest experience and boosting sales. By asking open-ended questions, making well-timed suggestions, and utilizing tools like menu design and CRM systems, you can guide guests toward choices that enhance their dining experience. Your expertise in these areas will help you connect with guests and create memorable, personalized interactions.

**Timing and Approach:**

* Ask open-ended questions to guide choices.  
* Make suggestions at appropriate moments.  
* Use the "Rule of Three" when offering options.  
* Employ the "Assumptive Close" technique.

**Product Knowledge:**

* Be thoroughly familiar with all menu items.  
* Highlight the value and quality of premium options.  
* Leverage seasonal items and limited-time offers.  
* Use social proof by mentioning popular choices.

**Personalization:**

* Utilize CRM systems to track guest preferences.  
* Stay customer-focused and adjust your approach.  
* Create urgency for special or limited items.  
* Use positive reinforcement for guest choices.

**Visual and Technological Aids:**

* Use menu design and table tents effectively.

### ⛔**What Not to Do in Suggestive Selling**

**When practicing suggestive selling, it’s crucial to avoid behaviors that can detract from the guest experience:**

1. Don't be pushy or aggressive.  
2. Avoid overwhelming guests with too many options.  
3. Don't ignore guest cues or body language.  
4. Never mislead about ingredients or preparation methods.  
5. Don't focus solely on the most expensive items.  
6. Avoid using negative language about menu items.  
7. Don't rush guests into making decisions.  
8. Never make guests feel judged for their choices.  
9. Don't neglect to mention allergies or dietary restrictions.  
10. Avoid interrupting conversations to make suggestions.

**Training and Motivation:**

1. Create a competitive environment among staff.  
2. Offer rewards for effective suggestive selling.  
3. Provide regular training on new menu items and techniques.  
4. Encourage staff to taste menu items for better descriptions.  
5. Share success stories and best practices among the team.

By following these guidelines and avoiding pitfalls, servers can master the art of suggestive selling, enhancing both the guest experience and the restaurant's bottom line.

### 👍**Customer Service Excellence**

Delivering exceptional customer service begins with the fundamentals. Greet guests promptly, ensuring they feel welcomed within one minute of being seated. Maintain strong eye contact, smile, and stand upright when interacting with guests—avoid leaning or writing on the table, as these actions can appear unprofessional. When taking orders, prioritize women and children first, demonstrating attentiveness and respect. These small yet significant actions help create a positive and memorable dining experience for every guest.

* **Greet Guests Promptly**: Welcome guests within one minute of being seated.  
* **Eye Contact and Body Language**: Maintain eye contact, smile, and stand straight when taking orders. Avoid leaning or writing on the table.  
* **Serve Women and Children First**: Prioritize taking orders from women and children before men.

### ⏱️**Order and Time Management**

Efficiently manage your time and tasks to ensure a smooth dining experience. Take accurate orders by knowing the menu well and check back with guests within 2 bites or 2 minutes. Serve water within one minute of seating, and handle glassware properly by avoiding contact with the rims. Consolidate trips to save time, especially during busy shifts, and use clear communication like “Corner\!” or “Behind\!” to navigate safely. These practices enhance service quality and efficiency.

* **Accurate Order Taking**: Be familiar with the menu, daily features, and prices. Always check back within 2 bites or 2 minutes after serving a course to ensure satisfaction.  
* **Water Service**: Ensure water is served within one minute of guests being seated.  
* **Handling Glassware**: Never touch the rim of drinking glasses or handle silverware over the eating surface.  
* **Consolidate Trips**: Use time wisely by minimizing trips and managing your time effectively during high-volume shifts.  
* **Train Yourself to Communicate**: Use phrases like “Corner\!” when navigating tight spaces and “Behind\!” when approaching from behind.

### 🎯**Six-Item Rule for Large Parties**

The **Six-Item Rule** is a best practice used to manage efficiency and accuracy when ringing in or serving large parties. It helps prevent service breakdowns by controlling how orders are communicated to the kitchen or bar.

#### **What It Is**

Never send more than 6 items on a single chit (order). Break large orders into smaller batches.

***Perspective:** If you have 10 guests and wait until the last person before hitting “send,” you’re delaying the kitchen and bar from starting any prep. That’s 3–5 minutes of lost time. Instead, use the system to your advantage:*

#### 

#### **When to Use**

* Parties of 8+ guests  
* Orders over 6 items total  
* Busy periods

#### **Why It Works**

* Prevents ticket chaos and kitchen/bar overload  
  * Dumping a 15-drink order on the bar at once clogs the well and slows the entire venue.  
* Reduces mistakes and re-fires  
* Allows proper timing between courses

### 🧽**Polishing & Pre-Busing**

### **Pre-Busing Tables** 

Maintaining a clean and organized table is crucial for delivering an exceptional dining experience. Pre-bussing refers to the practice of clearing used or empty dishes, glasses, and utensils from the table before the guests have finished their meal. This not only helps keep the table tidy but also ensures that guests have ample space for the next course, enhancing their overall dining experience.

#### **Key Steps in Pre-Bussing:**

1. **Monitor the Table:** Keep a close eye on the tables to promptly clear any dishes or items that are no longer in use. This includes empty plates, used cutlery, and discarded napkins.  
2. **Engage with Guests:** Before removing any items, discreetly ask the guests if they are finished with their dishes to avoid prematurely clearing something they may still want.  
3. **Clear Strategically:** Start by removing smaller items like empty glasses or side plates, followed by larger plates and utensils. This minimizes disruption to the guests’ meal and conversation.  
4. **Wipe Down Surfaces:** After clearing, quickly wipe down any spills or crumbs to maintain a clean surface. This step is essential for creating a seamless transition between courses.  
5. **Prepare for the Next Course:** Pre-bussing sets the stage for the next course, ensuring that the table is ready and guests can continue enjoying their meal without unnecessary delays.

#### **Benefits of Pre-Bussing:**

* **Enhanced Dining Experience:** A clean and organized table contributes to a pleasant dining environment, making guests feel more comfortable and valued.  
* **Efficiency:** Pre-bussing allows servers to manage their workload more effectively, reducing the time needed to fully clear and reset the table between courses or after the guests leave.  
* **Positive Impressions:** Guests are more likely to leave with a favorable impression of the establishment when their dining space is consistently maintained.

## 💵Closing Out a Table

---

### 🎯**Objective & Key Points**

#### **The process of closing out a table is crucial for leaving a lasting positive impression on your guests. Follow these professional guidelines to ensure a smooth and courteous table closure:**

1\. **Review the Check:** Carefully review the check before presenting it to ensure all items are accurately charged.

2\. **Cash Payments:** Process cash payments at the server station, not at the table. Return change on a tip tray or in a checkbook, and count change twice for accuracy.

3\. **Credit Card Payments:** Follow house procedures for processing credit card payments, and thank the guest without examining the tip.

4\. **Positive Final Impression:** Thank guests by name if possible and invite them back for another visit.

5\. **Accepted Payment Methods:** Cash, Visa, MasterCard, Discover, and American Express.

6\. **Handling Cash and Credit Card Slips:** Keep them securely in your wallet or apron, and make drops with a manager if carrying large amounts.

7\. **Checkout Procedures:** Use the computerized system to close out checks and keep all printed closed guest checks.

8\. **Pre-Checkout Checklist:** Ensure guests are finished, complete side work and closing duties, and maintain full dress attire.

9\. **Final Checkout:** Verify all checks and tips, print the closing report, and prepare cash due in the largest denominations possible.

By adhering to these steps, you ensure a professional and seamless table closure, leaving guests with a positive final impression of their dining experience.

## ✅Quality Control

---

### 🔑**Key Quality Control Checks**

Quality control is a primary responsibility of every employee serving food and drinks to guests. As the last point of contact before the guest, you play a crucial role in ensuring the quality of the dining experience.

### **General Guidelines:**

* If something doesn't look right or isn't presentable, **DO NOT SERVE IT.**  
* Remember: "People eat with their eyes." Ensure all products look appealing on the plates.  
* Double-check that food and drinks are 100% presentable and ready to eat.  
* Don't rush and overlook quality checks.

### **Key Quality Control Checks:**

1. **Temperature:**  
   * Ensure hot food is served hot and cold food is served cold.  
   * If food isn't at the proper temperature, DO NOT TAKE IT OUT. Inform the manager.  
2. **Cleanliness:**  
   * Check all plates, mugs, silverware, and napkins before presenting them to guests.  
3. **Portions and Accuracy:**  
   * Verify correct portions and ensure you're taking the right order to the right table.  
   * Ask yourself if you would eat the item if it were served to you.  
4. **Drink Presentation:**  
   * Ensure glasses are clean, filled to the proper level, and have correct, fresh garnishes.  
   * Avoid filling too soon to prevent condensation on the outside of the glass.  
5. **Condiments and Accompaniments:**  
   * Bring necessary condiments with the order (e.g., ketchup, mustard, butter, pepper).  
6. **Follow-up:**  
   * Check back within a minute or two to ensure everything is perfect.  
   * Use specific, sincere questions rather than generic ones like "Is everything ok?"

### ⚠️**Handling Issues**

Effectively managing guest complaints is essential for maintaining a positive dining experience, even when issues arise. When a guest expresses a concern, follow these key steps to resolve the situation professionally:

* **Apologize** sincerely to acknowledge the guest’s dissatisfaction.  
* **Remove problematic items immediately** to address the issue promptly.  
* **Get a manager involved** if necessary to ensure the situation is handled appropriately.  
* **Never argue with a customer or tell them they’re wrong**; always remain calm and respectful.

By following these guidelines, you can turn a potentially negative experience into a positive one, reinforcing your commitment to excellent customer service.
