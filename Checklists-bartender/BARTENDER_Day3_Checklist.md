## TRAINING CHECKLIST

### 1. Bar Opening Procedures (Lead)
- [ ] Trainee completes the full opening checklist independently. Trainer observes, corrects after.
- [ ] Stocking, ice, garnish prep, well organization. All done without prompting.
- [ ] "By now you should know the sequence. Show me."

### 2. Customer Service & Guest Engagement
- [ ] Key service etiquette: attentiveness, body language, tone, pacing. What separates good from forgettable.
- [ ] The 5-10 rule, eye contact, confident welcoming language
- [ ] Trainee greets every guest first today. Trainer observes and coaches after each interaction.
- [ ] "You're not just making drinks. You're hosting."

### 3. Upselling: Fundamentals & Product Knowledge
- [ ] Ethical upselling: suggest the upgrade, don't push the sale. Enhance the experience, not the check.
- [ ] Review premium products (spirits, specialty cocktails) and practice assessing guest preferences
- [ ] Trainee recommends based on what the guest describes. Trainer evaluates the approach, not just the pick.
- [ ] "Knowledge is not power. Actionable and executed knowledge is power."

### 4. High-Volume Mindset & Strategy
- [ ] Multitasking, prioritization, maintaining quality when it gets busy
- [ ] Controlling the pace: you run the bar, the bar doesn't run you
- [ ] Strategies for staying composed under pressure. Don't let the animals run the zoo.
- [ ] "Work smart, stay organized, move with purpose."

### 5. Live Service Practice & Guest Scenarios
- [ ] Trainee works the bar: greets guests, takes orders, builds drinks. Trainer supervises.
- [ ] All cocktails made with jigger, trainer approval before serving
- [ ] Trainer introduces real-time scenarios: difficult guest, wrong order, complaint about drink strength
- [ ] Trainee handles each situation, trainer debriefs after. Focus on composure, not perfection.

### 6. Cross-Training Expo Session #1 *(Restaurant locations only)*
- [ ] Expo station setup, expediting food orders, quality control, communication with BOH
- [ ] Trainee works expo 30-45 min. Goal: understand how BOH impacts the bar and vice versa.
- [ ] Once complete, trainee returns to assigned trainer for remainder of shift

### 7. Technical Skills Practice (End of Shift)
- [ ] Free-pour practice: pour count knowledge, jigger use, single vs. double pours. Trainer measures after each pour.
- [ ] Shaking and stirring: trainee builds mocktails under supervision. Focus on consistency and speed improvement from Day 2.
- [ ] Accuracy is still the priority. Speed is earned.

### 8. Continued Education & Memorization (End of Shift: 30 min)
- [ ] Classic cocktails, shots, and venue-specific specialty drinks
- [ ] Trainer quizzes verbally. Note gaps for Day 4.
- [ ] "Recipe knowledge separates average from great. Memorize."
