## TRAINING CHECKLIST

### 1. Bar Opening Procedures
- [ ] Walk through the full opening checklist with trainee
- [ ] Trainee practices setup: stocking, ice, garnish prep, well organization
- [ ] "A clean, stocked bar before service starts is non-negotiable."

### 2. POS System Orientation (Toast)
- [ ] Log trainee into Toast, set up their profile and PIN
- [ ] Walk through the menu layout: categories, modifiers, special instructions, coursing

### 3. POS Hands-On Practice
- [ ] Trainee rings in mock orders: cocktails, beer, wine, shots, with modifications
- [ ] Practice opening tabs, closing tabs, splitting checks, transferring tabs

### 4. POS Troubleshooting
- [ ] Cover common issues: printer jams, duplicate tickets, declined cards, voiding items
- [ ] Who to call and when to get the MOD involved

### 5. Jigger Types & Proper Pouring Techniques
- [ ] Show different jigger sizes (0.5oz, 0.75oz, 1oz, 1.5oz, 2oz)
- [ ] Demonstrate proper jigger hold, fill level, and pour technique
- [ ] "Consistency starts with the jigger. Every pour, every time."

### 6. Measuring Techniques
- [ ] Under-pouring vs. over-pouring: what it costs the business and what it does to the drink
- [ ] Precision matters for quality AND cost control

### 7. Building a Cocktail
- [ ] Walk through cocktail structure: base spirit, modifier, sweetener, acid, garnish
- [ ] Trainer builds a cocktail while explaining each component and why it's there
- [ ] Trainee builds one alongside the trainer with guidance

### 8. Hands-On Tool Familiarization
- [ ] Trainee handles every tool: shaker tin, mixing glass, bar spoon, muddler, strainers, peeler, pour spouts
- [ ] Practice the motion: shake, stir, strain, pour
- [ ] "Everything has a home. Put it back."

### 9. Introduction to Shaking Techniques
- [ ] When to shake: citrus, cream, egg white, anything that needs aeration
- [ ] Demonstrate grip, motion, timing (8-10 seconds standard)
- [ ] Trainee practices with ice and water

### 10. Introduction to Stirring Techniques
- [ ] When to stir: spirit-forward cocktails (Manhattan, Negroni, Old Fashioned)
- [ ] Demonstrate proper bar spoon technique, smooth rotation, minimal splash
- [ ] Trainee practices stirring for 20-30 seconds, checking dilution

### 11. Pouring Orientation
- [ ] Introduction to free pouring, pour counts, and when to use a jigger vs. free pour
- [ ] Standard count: 4-count = 1.5oz (venue standard)
- [ ] "Speed is earned. Accuracy comes first."

### 12. Bottle Holding Techniques
- [ ] Single bottle hold: grip, angle, pour control
- [ ] Multi-bottle hold: managing two bottles in one hand
- [ ] Demonstrate during both free pour and jigger pour scenarios

### 13. Free-Pour Practice (30 min, end of shift)
- [ ] Set up test bottles with water and measuring tools
- [ ] Trainee practices pour counts, checks accuracy with jigger after each pour
- [ ] Calibrate: adjust count if consistently over or under
- [ ] "Every ounce matters. Over-pouring kills margins. Under-pouring kills the drink."

### 14. Shadow & Observe (1 hour)
- [ ] Trainee stands behind the bar watching trainer during live service
- [ ] Focus areas: ticket flow, guest interaction, multitasking, drink sequencing
- [ ] Trainer narrates decisions in real time when possible
- [ ] "Watch how I move. Tomorrow you start doing this."

### 15. Classic Cocktail & Shot Questionnaire (10 min, end of shift)
- [ ] Verbal quiz: trainer asks trainee to name ingredients for at least 5 classic cocktails and 5 shots
- [ ] Ad-lib format. Gauge retention, not perfection. Note gaps for Day 3.
- [ ] If applicable: review venue-specific specialty cocktails
