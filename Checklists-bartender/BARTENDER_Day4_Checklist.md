## TRAINING CHECKLIST

### 1. Cross-Training Expo Session #2 *(Restaurant locations only)*
- [ ] Trainee manages the expo window with more independence than Session #1
- [ ] Food order coordination, quality control, communication with BOH
- [ ] Once complete, trainee returns to assigned trainer for remainder of shift

### 2. Responsible Alcohol Service
- [ ] Local/state alcohol laws, underage policies, proper ID checking. Trainee must know the legal line.
- [ ] Signs of intoxication: slurred speech, loss of balance, aggression, slow response
- [ ] Strategies for slowing service: suggest food/water, pace drinks, stop serving when necessary
- [ ] Refusing service professionally. Stay calm, stay firm, never say "you're drunk."
- [ ] When to involve MOD: any pushback, any escalation, any doubt. This protects the guest, the trainee, and the business.

### 3. Mise En Place & Station Setup
- [ ] Trainee sets up their own station before anything else. No guidance. Trainer evaluates after.
- [ ] Tools, garnishes, juices, syrups, glassware, ice, towels. All within reach, all in order.
- [ ] Station must be service-ready before moving on. This is a Day 4 expectation, not a lesson.

### 4. Garnish Knowledge & Ingredients
- [ ] Types of garnishes: wheels, wedges, twists, peels, herbs, olives, cherries, specialty
- [ ] Tools: Y-peeler, citrus peeler, channel knife, paring knife. Trainee names and demonstrates each.
- [ ] Syrups, purees, mixers, bitters. What's available, how each affects cocktail balance.
- [ ] Storage and maintenance: FIFO, shelf life, labeling, refrigeration. Fresh product makes better drinks.

### 5. Garnishing Techniques & Drink Presentation
- [ ] Trainee cuts and preps all garnishes for service. Trainer evaluates speed, technique, and consistency.
- [ ] Trainee garnishes 5+ cocktails. Standard: same garnish, same size, same placement, every time.
- [ ] Full drink presentation: garnish, ice level, glassware, napkin. Nothing leaves the bar sloppy.
- [ ] A well-presented drink reinforces the brand. A sloppy one undermines everything else you did right.

### 6. Glass Care & Polishing *(Location based)*
- [ ] Proper handling, cleaning, polishing. Never grab by the rim.
- [ ] Breakage protocol: how to handle it safely, who to notify, how to replace
- [ ] Spotless glassware is non-negotiable. If it's not clean, it doesn't leave the bar.

### 7. Live Service: Speed, Efficiency & Multitasking
- [ ] Trainee works the bar during live service. Trainer shadows and coaches in real time.
- [ ] Trainer fires tickets, trainee manages the build queue and guest interaction simultaneously
- [ ] Focus: movement economy, sequencing similar builds, keeping the rail clean, no wasted steps
- [ ] This is the real test before Day 5. Composure under volume matters more than raw speed.

### 8. POS System Review
- [ ] Complex scenarios: splitting checks, large group tabs, comps, voids, discounts
- [ ] Trainee handles all POS operations for the remainder of the shift
- [ ] By now, Toast should feel like muscle memory. If it doesn't, flag it.
