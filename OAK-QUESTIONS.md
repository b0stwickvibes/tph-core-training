## Bartender Category 4: Bar Service & High-Volume Execution

31. **What is the “Hi!” Method timing for a new bar guest?**  
   Within 10 seconds of them sitting down.

32. **How do you acknowledge a guest while mid-drink build?**  
   Immediate eye contact and a verbal acknowledgment without abandoning the current task.

33. **What is the Silent Hero technique for bar service?**  
   Refill or reset without interrupting the guest’s flow.

34. **When should you offer a refill?**  
   When the glass is about one-third full.

35. **How do you pitch the $150 Mega Mule?**  
   Position it as a premium shared experience for groups, celebrations, and entertainment value.

36. **What words should be used when pitching the Mega Mule?**  
   Sharing, premium, experience, large group, celebration, special occasion.

37. **What should you do if the group declines the Mega Mule?**  
   Offer alternative cocktail recommendations confidently.

38. **How would you pair a beer with Wings for a guest who wants balance with spice?**  
   Recommend a lighter, crisp beer that helps cleanse the palate.

## Bartender Category 5: Situational Logic & Safety

39. **You are low on limes mid-rush. What is the protocol?**  
   Communicate early with the bar team, barback, or manager before running out.

40. **A guest sends a drink back. How do you handle it?**  
   Apologize, remake it or guide them to a better option, and never argue.

41. **Another bartender is in the weeds. What is your first move?**  
   Ask what they need most if your own station is under control.

42. **A guest looks intoxicated but asks for a double. What do you do?**  
   Politely decline, offer water or food, and involve a manager.

43. **The POS system goes down. What do you do?**  
   Attempt a basic reset, notify management immediately, and follow backup procedure.

44. **Glass breaks in your ice well. What are the immediate steps?**  
   Stop using that well and burn the well immediately.

45. **A guest complains about a $15 signature cocktail price. What do you say?**  
   Explain the value through premium spirits and fresh ingredients, then guide them to a lower-priced option if needed.

46. **You notice a coworker over-pouring. What should you do?**  
   Address it immediately through proper escalation and protect cost control standards.

## Server Category 3: Consultative Selling & Upselling

31. **Define consultative selling.**  
   Acting as a trusted guide who helps guests choose what they will enjoy most.

32. **What is a Choice Between Yesses?**  
   Offering two strong options instead of a yes/no question.

33. **When is the best time to suggest an appetizer?**  
   During the initial beverage order.

34. **Why is the number three effective for recommendations?**  
   It gives the guest enough choice without feeling pushy or limiting.

35. **How would you upsell a guest ordering a Whiskey Ginger?**  
   Recommend a premium bourbon such as Angel’s Envy for a smoother profile.

36. **How would you upsell a guest ordering the Pretzel?**  
   Recommend Whipped Feta as a second shared snack.

37. **How do you pitch the Mac + Cheese or Fingerling Mash as an add-on?**  
   Position it as a must-have side or shared addition that rounds out the meal.

38. **When should you seed the dessert menu?**  
   During the 2-bite / 2-minute check or later in the meal flow when appropriate.

39. **How can you help a guest choose between entrées?**  
   Ask targeted questions about what they are in the mood for.

40. **A guest wants “a few snacks.” What is a strong upsell move?**  
   Add a premium side or second shared snack to round out the order.

## Server Category 4: Efficiency, Prioritization & Floor Logic

41. **What is the Triple Threat priority sequence?**  
   1. Hot Food  
   2. Guest Needs  
   3. Interaction / Sidework

42. **What does Full Hands In / Full Hands Out mean?**  
   Never enter or leave the kitchen empty-handed.

43. **What is the 30-Second Assessment?**  
   Quickly reading table energy, pace, and intent to adapt service style.

44. **How do you handle Table 1 needing a refill, Table 2 with hot food, and Table 3 wanting to hear about cocktails?**  
   Run hot food first, then handle immediate guest need, then return for the sales conversation.

45. **You are clearing a Pretzel board, the host is seating a new party, and a guest at the bar signals for water. What do you do?**  
   Acknowledge immediately, complete the current high-efficiency movement, then return in the correct sequence without ignoring anyone.

46. **A guest says their Wings are too naked. How do you resolve it?**  
   Satisfy the guest immediately, communicate with the kitchen, and keep the guest updated.

47. **What is the Silent Hero technique for table maintenance?**  
   Quietly removing clutter and resetting the table without disrupting the guest experience.

48. **What is the 10-Foot Rule for all OAK staff?**  
   Within 10 feet of a guest, make eye contact and acknowledge them.

49. **What is the goal of proactive service?**  
   Anticipate guest needs before they ask.

50. **How do you use guest names to build rapport?**  
   Use them naturally when provided.
