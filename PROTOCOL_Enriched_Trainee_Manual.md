# Enriched Trainee Manual — Content Protocol

---

## Purpose

This document defines the rules for writing enriched trainee content in Trainual. All content is **post-training reinforcement** — material a trainee reviews after their shift to lock in what they practiced on the floor.

## Category Page Template

Each category page follows this structure:

```
# [Category Name]

> Debrief block (What This Covers / Checklist Steps / Learning Focus)

---

## Step X: [Title] `[Bucket]`

[Content per bucket rules below]

---

## Step Y: [Title] `[Bucket]`

[Content per bucket rules below]

---

# Conclusion
# Knowledge Check
```

---

## Debrief Block

Every category opens with a debrief. Keep it tight:

```
> **What This Covers:** [1–2 sentences — what this group of steps is about]
>
> **Checklist Steps:** [step numbers]
>
> **Learning Focus:** [what the trainee should understand after reading]
```

---

## Bucket Selection Rules

**One bucket per step. No mixing.**

| If the step teaches... | Use this bucket |
|------------------------|-----------------|
| Physical bartending execution (pouring, shaking, building) | **Skill** |
| Venue systems or workflow (setup, layout, POS, closing) | **Process** |
| Guest interaction or service behavior (greeting, upselling, handling situations) | **Guest-Facing** |

---

## Bucket Templates

### Skill
```
- **Spec:** The non-negotiable rule or standard
- **Metrics:** Specific measurements, counts, or targets
- **Pro Tip:** One efficiency or quality shortcut
- **Misses:** The most common mistake to avoid
```

### Process
```
- **Objective:** Why this system exists (one sentence)
- **Audit:** What to check or verify
- **Reset:** How to organize/prepare for the next person
- **Result:** What it looks like when done right
```

### Guest-Facing
```
- **The Approach:** How to initiate the interaction
- **Energy:** The vibe or tone expected
- **Scenarios:** A real-world example
- **Pitfalls:** A behavioral mistake to avoid
```

---

## Priority Logic: When to Use Bullets vs. Sentences

**Not every step needs 4 bullets.**

### Use full bucket (3–4 bullets) when:
- The step has multiple operational takeaways
- There are specific standards, metrics, or common mistakes
- The trainee needs to scan for quick reference

### Use 1–2 sentence reinforcement when:
- The step is singular (e.g., a tour, a shadowing session, meeting the team)
- The takeaway is one idea, not multiple operational points
- Forcing bullets would create filler

**Example of sentence-only step:**

```
## Step 6: The Grand Tour `[Process]`

Your trainer walked you through FOH and BOH. You should now be able to
navigate to any area (bar stations, walk-ins, dish pit, exits) without
asking. If you can't point to it, ask again tomorrow.
```

---

## Compression Rules

### Remove:
- Long explanations
- Trainer narration ("Your trainer will...")
- Motivational language ("You've got this!")
- Storytelling or history lessons
- Redundant phrasing

### Keep:
- The operational idea
- The standard or spec
- The takeaway from the shift

---

## The Retention Test

Every bullet must answer one question:

> **"What should the trainee remember from practicing this today?"**

If the bullet does not reinforce a real takeaway from the shift → **remove it.**

---

## Formatting Rules

### Headers
- **H1** = Day title (`# Day 1: Orientation`)
- **H1** = Category name (`# The Standard`)
- **H2** = Step title (`## Step 1: Welcome & Introduction`)
- **H2** = Objectives / Conclusion / Next Steps sub-headers

### Bucket Tags
- Inline code after step title: `` `[Skill]` `` / `` `[Process]` `` / `` `[Guest-Facing]` ``

### Step Descriptors
- Bold one-liner under the step title describing what the step covers
- This comes from the floor checklist's step description

### Bullets
- Bold label followed by colon and content
- Maximum 4 bullets per step (bucket headers only)
- No sub-bullets. No nested lists. Flat structure only.

### Debrief Blocks
- Use blockquote (`>`) formatting
- Three fields only: What This Covers, Checklist Steps, Learning Focus

### Em Dashes
- **Do not use em dashes (—) in trainee-facing content.**
- Use colons for introductions or lists, periods for sentence breaks, semicolons for connected thoughts, commas for light pauses, and parentheses for parenthetical asides.
- This keeps Trainual content clean across all devices and export formats.

## Conclusion Page Template

```
# Conclusion

[2–3 sentences — what the trainee should now be able to do after this day]

## Next Steps

- [Action item 1 — usually "Complete Knowledge Check"]
- [Action item 2 — usually "Review [specific material]"]
- [Action item 3 — usually "Prepare for [Day X+1 topic]"]

| ⚠️ *[Warning or reminder about Day X+1 quiz/expectations]* |
| :---: |
```

## Quality Checklist (Before Submitting Content)

- [ ] Every step passes the Retention Test
- [ ] No trainer narration or motivational filler
- [ ] Each step uses exactly one bucket (or sentence-only format)
- [ ] Bullets are flat — no sub-bullets, no nesting
- [ ] Simple steps use 1–2 sentences, not forced bullets
- [ ] Debrief block appears at top of every category
- [ ] H1/H2 hierarchy is correct
- [ ] Step numbering matches the floor checklist
